ॐ नमो नारायणाय
AUM namo nArAyaNAya

अथ श्रीमन्नारायणीयम्
atha shrImannArAyaNIyam

And now the highly auspicious shlokas of Narayana

त्रयोविंशम् दशकम्
trayoviMsham dashakam

The Twenty-third group of Ten shlokas

दक्ष चरितम् चित्रकेतु उपाख्यानम् च
dakSha charitam chitraketu upAkhyAnam cha

The Story of Daksha and Chitraketu

प्राचेतसस्तु भगवन्नपरो हि दक्ष:
त्वत्सेवनं व्यधित सर्गविवृद्धिकाम: ।
आविर्बभूविथ तदा लसदष्टबाहु:
तस्मै वरं ददिथ तां च वधूमसिक्नीम् ॥१॥
prAchetasastu bhagavannaparo hi dakShaH
tvatsevanaM vyadhita sargavivRddhikAmaH 
AvirbabhUvitha tadA lasadaShTabAhuH
tasmai varaM daditha tAM cha vadhUmasiknIm 1

प्राचेतसस्तु भगवन्नपरो हि दक्ष:
O Lord! Of the Prachetas, there was a son, who too was named Daksha,
त्वत्सेवनं व्यधित सर्गविवृद्धिकाम: ।
who performed your worship, desirous of furthering creation!
आविर्बभूविथ तदा लसदष्टबाहु:
You appeared in front of him, resplendent with eight arms,
तस्मै वरं ददिथ तां च वधूमसिक्नीम् ॥१॥
granted him the boon, and a wife by the name of Asikni.

तस्यात्मजास्त्वयुतमीश पुनस्सहस्रं
श्रीनारदस्य वचसा तव मार्गमापु: ।
नैकत्रवासमृषये स मुमोच शापं
भक्तोत्तमस्त्वृषिरनुग्रहमेव मेने ॥२॥
tasyAtmajAstvayutamIsha punassahasraM
shrInAradasya vachasA tava mArgamApuH 
naikatravAsamRShaye sa mumocha shApaM
bhaktottamastvRShiranugrahameva mene 2

तस्यात्मजास्त्वयुतमीश पुनस्सहस्रं
His sons, O Lord! Ten thousand and one more thousand,
श्रीनारदस्य वचसा तव मार्गमापु: ।
guided by the words of the auspicious Narada, took to your path of liberation!
नैकत्रवासमृषये स मुमोच शापं
Angered, Daksha cursed Narada that he would never have a residence,
भक्तोत्तमस्त्वृषिरनुग्रहमेव मेने ॥२॥
he, the best of devotees however took it as a blessing itself!

षष्ट्या ततो दुहितृभि: सृजत: कुलौघान्
दौहित्रसूनुरथ तस्य स विश्वरूप: ।
त्वत्स्तोत्रवर्मितमजापयदिन्द्रमाजौ
देव त्वदीयमहिमा खलु सर्वजैत्र: ॥३॥
ShaShTyA tato duhitRbhiH sRjataH kulaughAn
dauhitrasUnuratha tasya sa vishvarUpaH 
tvatstotravarmitamajApayadindramAjau
deva tvadIyamahimA khalu sarvajaitraH 3

षष्ट्या ततो दुहितृभि: सृजत: कुलौघान्
With Sixty of his daughters, he continued building his lineage.
दौहित्रसूनुरथ तस्य स विश्वरूप: ।
One of the sons of his daughters, by the name of Vishvarupa,
त्वत्स्तोत्रवर्मितमजापयदिन्द्रमाजौ
as he was sought protection by Indra, made him chant a hymn that grants your protection!
देव त्वदीयमहिमा खलु सर्वजैत्र: ॥३॥
O Lord! Isn't your grace the one that wins over everything!

प्राक्शूरसेनविषये किल चित्रकेतु:
पुत्राग्रही नृपतिरङ्गिरस: प्रभावात् ।
लब्ध्वैकपुत्रमथ तत्र हते सपत्नी-
सङ्घैरमुह्यदवशस्तव माययासौ ॥४॥
prAkshUrasenaviShaye kila chitraketuH
putrAgrahI nRpatirangirasaH prabhAvAt 
labdhvaikaputramatha tatra hate sapatnI-
sanghairamuhyadavashastava mAyayAsau 4

प्राक्शूरसेनविषये किल चित्रकेतु:
Once upon a time, in the kingdom of Shurasena, hasn't King Chitraketu,
पुत्राग्रही नृपतिरङ्गिरस: प्रभावात् ।
desirous of sons, under the influence of sage Angiras,
लब्ध्वैकपुत्रमथ तत्र हते सपत्नी-
got a son, and when he was killed by his step-wife
सङ्घैरमुह्यदवशस्तव माययासौ ॥४॥
groups, overcome with grief, fainted owing to the power of your Maya!

तं नारदस्तु सममङ्गिरसा दयालु:
सम्प्राप्य तावदुपदर्श्य सुतस्य जीवम् ।
कस्यास्मि पुत्र इति तस्य गिरा विमोहं
त्यक्त्वा त्वदर्चनविधौ नृपतिं न्ययुङ्क्त ॥५॥
taM nAradastu samamangirasA dayAluH
samprApya tAvadupadarshya sutasya jIvam 
kasyAsmi putra iti tasya girA vimohaM
tyaktvA tvadarchanavidhau nRpatiM nyayunkta 5

तं नारदस्तु सममङ्गिरसा दयालु:
Him, Narada, who was as merciful as Angiras,
सम्प्राप्य तावदुपदर्श्य सुतस्य जीवम् ।
approached, and showed the soul of his son!
कस्यास्मि पुत्र इति तस्य गिरा विमोहं
"Whose son am I" that soul said, and with those words,
त्यक्त्वा त्वदर्चनविधौ नृपतिं न्ययुङ्क्त ॥५॥
he gave up delusion, and the sages instructed him in the ways of your worship.

स्तोत्रं च मन्त्रमपि नारदतोऽथ लब्ध्वा
तोषाय शेषवपुषो ननु ते तपस्यन् ।
विद्याधराधिपतितां स हि सप्तरात्रे
लब्ध्वाप्यकुण्ठमतिरन्वभजद्भवन्तम् ॥६॥
stotraM cha mantramapi nAradatotha labdhvA
toShAya sheShavapuSho nanu te tapasyan 
vidyAdharAdhipatitAM sa hi saptarAtre
labdhvApyakuNThamatiranvabhajadbhavantam 6

स्तोत्रं च मन्त्रमपि नारदतोऽथ लब्ध्वा
Having received your stotras and mantras from Narada,
तोषाय शेषवपुषो ननु ते तपस्यन् ।
in order to please you in the form of Adishesha, he perfomed penance,
विद्याधराधिपतितां स हि सप्तरात्रे
and got leadership of Vidyadharas within seven nights!
लब्ध्वाप्यकुण्ठमतिरन्वभजद्भवन्तम् ॥६॥
Despite getting it, still with his mind unfulfilled, he continued your prayer!

तस्मै मृणालधवलेन सहस्रशीर्ष्णा
रूपेण बद्धनुतिसिद्धगणावृतेन ।
प्रादुर्भवन्नचिरतो नुतिभि: प्रसन्नो
दत्वाऽऽत्मतत्त्वमनुगृह्य तिरोदधाथ ॥७॥
tasmai mRNAladhavalena sahasrashIrShNA
rUpeNa baddhanutisiddhagaNAvRtena 
prAdurbhavannachirato nutibhiH prasanno
datvAtmatattvamanugRhya tirodadhAtha 7

तस्मै मृणालधवलेन सहस्रशीर्ष्णा
To him, with the purity of a lotus stalk, with a thousand headed
रूपेण बद्धनुतिसिद्धगणावृतेन ।
form, worshipped by the hymns of groups of sages surrounding you,
प्रादुर्भवन्नचिरतो नुतिभि: प्रसन्नो
you manifested in no time, having been pleased by his prayers!
दत्वाऽऽत्मतत्त्वमनुगृह्य तिरोदधाथ ॥७॥
Granting him the knowledge of the self and blessing him, you disappeared!

त्वद्भक्तमौलिरथ सोऽपि च लक्षलक्षं
वर्षाणि हर्षुलमना भुवनेषु कामम् ।
सङ्गापयन् गुणगणं तव सुन्दरीभि:
सङ्गातिरेकरहितो ललितं चचार ॥८॥
tvadbhaktamauliratha sopi cha lakShalakShaM
varShANi harShulamanA bhuvaneShu kAmam 
sangApayan guNagaNaM tava sundarIbhiH
sangAtirekarahito lalitaM chachAra 8

त्वद्भक्तमौलिरथ सोऽपि च लक्षलक्षं
Then, that great devotee of yours, for lakhs and lakhs
वर्षाणि हर्षुलमना भुवनेषु कामम् ।
of years, with a joyous mind, in all the worlds, to his desires content
सङ्गापयन् गुणगणं तव सुन्दरीभि:
singing the praise of your groups of attributes, along with beautiful Vidyadhara women,
सङ्गातिरेकरहितो ललितं चचार ॥८॥
living wihout the influence of sensual attachment, gracefully wandered about!

अत्यन्तसङ्गविलयाय भवत्प्रणुन्नो
नूनं स रूप्यगिरिमाप्य महत्समाजे ।
निश्शङ्कमङ्ककृतवल्लभमङ्गजारिं
तं शङ्करं परिहसन्नुमयाभिशेपे ॥९॥
atyantasangavilayAya bhavatpraNunno
nUnaM sa rUpyagirimApya mahatsamAje 
nishshankamankakRtavallabhamangajAriM
taM shankaraM parihasannumayAbhishepe 9

अत्यन्तसङ्गविलयाय भवत्प्रणुन्नो
To rid himself completely of all attachment, prompted by you,
नूनं स रूप्यगिरिमाप्य महत्समाजे ।
he reached the silvery mountain(kailAsa), and in the company of great sages,
निश्शङ्कमङ्ककृतवल्लभमङ्गजारिं
without any hesitation as Shiva was seating Parvati in his lap,
तं शङ्करं परिहसन्नुमयाभिशेपे ॥९॥
he made fun of Shankara, and due to that, got cursed by Uma!

निस्सम्भ्रमस्त्वयमयाचितशापमोक्षो
वृत्रासुरत्वमुपगम्य सुरेन्द्रयोधी ।
भक्त्यात्मतत्त्वकथनै: समरे विचित्रं
शत्रोरपि भ्रममपास्य गत: पदं ते ॥१०॥
nissambhramastvayamayAchitashApamokSho
vRtrAsuratvamupagamya surendrayodhI 
bhaktyAtmatattvakathanaiH samare vichitraM
shatrorapi bhramamapAsya gataH padaM te 10

निस्सम्भ्रमस्त्वयमयाचितशापमोक्षो
Undisturbed, and not asking for any relief from the curse,
वृत्रासुरत्वमुपगम्य सुरेन्द्रयोधी ।
he turned into the form of Vritrasura, who fought Lord Indra.
भक्त्यात्मतत्त्वकथनै: समरे विचित्रं
With his devotion, imparting the knowledge of the self during battle, in a wondrous way,
शत्रोरपि भ्रममपास्य गत: पदं ते ॥१०॥
even of his enemy, removing delusion, he attained to your abode! 

त्वत्सेवनेन दितिरिन्द्रवधोद्यताऽपि
तान्प्रत्युतेन्द्रसुहृदो मरुतोऽभिलेभे ।
दुष्टाशयेऽपि शुभदैव भवन्निषेवा
तत्तादृशस्त्वमव मां पवनालयेश ॥११॥
tvatsevanena ditirindravadhodyatApi
tAnpratyutendrasuhRdo marutobhilebhe 
duShTAshayepi shubhadaiva bhavanniShevA
tattAdRshastvamava mAM pavanAlayesha 11

त्वत्सेवनेन दितिरिन्द्रवधोद्यताऽपि
By worshipping you, Diti who was so intent on Indra's death,
तान्प्रत्युतेन्द्रसुहृदो मरुतोऽभिलेभे ।
begot Maruts as her sons, who later turned into Indra's friends!
दुष्टाशयेऽपि शुभदैव भवन्निषेवा
Even if performed with an evil intent, your worship always bestows well-being!
तत्तादृशस्त्वमव मां पवनालयेश ॥११॥
O Lord who is of such nature, protect me, O Lord of the Abode of Wind!
