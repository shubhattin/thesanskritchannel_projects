अथ श्री विज्ञानभैरव तन्त्रम् 
atha shrI vijnAnabhairava tantram

And now, the 112 techniques of dissolution
revealed by Bhairava to Devi!

श्री देव्युवाच ।
shrI dEvyuvAcha 

The auspicious Goddess spoke...

श्रुतं देव मया सर्वं रुद्रयामलसम्भवम् ।
त्रिकभेदमशेषेण सारात्सारविभागशः ॥ १ ॥
shrutaM dEva mayA sarvaM rudrayAmalasambhavam
trikabhEdamashEShENa sArAtsAravibhAgashaH  1

O Deva, I have heard completely all that arose from the Rudra Yamala Tantra,
including the divisions of the Trika system in their entirety, explained through distinctions of its essence.

अद्यापि न निवृत्तो मे संशयः परमेश्वर ।
किं रूपं तत्त्वतो देव शब्दराशिकलामयम् ॥ २ ॥
adyApi na nivRttO mE saMshayaH paramEshvara 
kiM rUpaM tattvatO dEva shabdarAshikalAmayam  2 

Yet even now, my doubt has not gone away, O Parameshvara.
What truly is your form, O Deva, which is made up of 
various kinds of vibrations, and attributes?

किं वा नवात्मभेदेन भैरवे भैरवाकृतौ ।
त्रिशिरोभेदभिन्नं वा किं वा शक्तित्रयात्मकम् ॥ ३ ॥
kiM vA navAtmabhEdEna bhairavE bhairavAkRtau 
trishirObhEdabhinnaM vA kiM vA shaktitrayAtmakam  3 

Or is Bhairava, in the form of Sound, divided into nine different forms?
Or is He differentiated first, as the Three-Headed One?
Or is He of the nature of the three powers?

नादबिन्दुमयं वापि किं चन्द्रार्धनिरोधिकाः ।
चक्रारूढमनच्कं वा किं वा शक्तिस्वरूपकम् ॥ ४ ॥
nAdabindumayaM vApi kiM chandrArdhanirOdhikAH 
chakrArUDhamanachkaM vA kiM vA shaktisvarUpakam  4 

Or is your form composed of Nāda and Bindu?
Or as the half-moon and the dot on top that stops it?
Or is it established within the chakras, yet beyond the chakras?
Or is it of the very nature of Shakti?

परापरायाः सकलमपरायाश्च वा पुनः ।
पराया यदि तद्वत्स्यात्परत्वं तद्विरुध्यते ॥ ५ ॥
parAparAyAH sakalamaparAyAshcha vA punaH 
parAyA yadi tadvatsyAtparatvaM tadvirudhyatE  5 

Is it the totality of manifest and the unmanifest,
or again completely of the Manifest?
If it were to be truly of the nature of the manifest,
then wouldn't its transcendence become contradicted?

न हि वर्णविभेदेन देहभेदेन वा भवेत् ।
परत्वं निष्कलत्वेन सकलत्वे न तद्भवेत् ॥ ६ ॥
na hi varNavibhEdEna dEhabhEdEna vA bhavEt 
paratvaM niShkalatvEna sakalatvE na tadbhavEt  6 

For transcendence does not arise when there are different kinds of qualities,
nor through different kinds of bodies.
True transcendence arises when there are no more attributes,
and cannot happen to that which possesses attributes.

प्रसादं कुरु मे नाथ निःशेषं छिन्द्धि संशयम् ।
prasAdaM kuru mE nAtha niHshEShaM Chinddhi saMshayam 

Grant me your grace, O Nātha.
Completely cut away my doubt.

भैरव उवाच ।
bhairava uvAcha 

Bhairava spoke:

साधु साधु त्वया पृष्टं तन्त्रसारमिदं प्रिये ॥ ७ ॥
sAdhu sAdhu tvayA pRShTaM tantrasAramidaM priyE  7 

Well asked, well asked by you, O Beloved.
This indeed is the essence of the Tantras.

गूहनीयतमं भद्रे तथापि कथयामि ते ।
यत्किंचित्सकलं रूपं भैरवस्य प्रकीर्तितम् ॥ ८ ॥
gUhanIyatamaM bhadrE tathApi kathayAmi tE 
yatkiMchitsakalaM rUpaM bhairavasya prakIrtitam  8 

Though this is the most secretive of teachings, O Auspicious One,
I shall still reveal it to you.
These are all the various forms of Bhairava that are widely renowned.

तदसारतया देवि विज्ञेयं शक्रजालवत् ।
मायास्वप्नोपमं चैव गन्धर्वनगरभ्रमम् ॥ ९ ॥
tadasAratayA dEvi vijnEyaM shakrajAlavat 
mAyAsvapnOpamaM chaiva gandharvanagarabhramam  9 

All of those, O Devi, should be understood as without substance,
like a magical illusion of Indra,
like Māyā, like a dream,
like the illusory city of the Gandharvas.

ध्यानार्थं भ्रान्तबुद्धीनां क्रियाडम्बरवर्तिनाम् ।
केवलं वर्णितं पुंसां विकल्पनिहतात्मनाम् ॥ १० ॥
dhyAnArthaM bhrAntabuddhInAM kriyADambaravartinAm 
kEvalaM varNitaM puMsAM vikalpanihatAtmanAm  10 

These descriptions are given only for the purpose of meditation,
for those whose intellects tend to wander,
who are engaged in elaborate external rituals, and
whose inner nature is overpowered by conceptual thought.

तत्त्वतो न नवात्मासौ शब्दराशिर्न भैरवः ।
न चासौ त्रिशिरा देवो न च शक्तित्रयात्मकः ॥ ११ ॥
tattvatO na navAtmAsau shabdarAshirna bhairavaH 
na chAsau trishirA dEvO na cha shaktitrayAtmakaH  11 

In truth, Bhairava is not the nine-fold Self,
nor is He a cluster of sounds.
That Deva is not the Three-Headed One,
nor is He of the nature of the three powers.

नादबिन्दुमयो वापि न चन्द्रार्धनिरोधिकाः ।
न चक्रक्रमसम्भिन्नो न च शक्तिस्वरूपकः ॥ १२ ॥
nAdabindumayO vApi na chandrArdhanirOdhikAH 
na chakrakramasambhinnO na cha shaktisvarUpakaH  12 

Neither is He composed of Nāda and Bindu,
nor the half-moon and Nirodhikā.
He is not divided through the sequence of chakras,
nor is He manifest as the form of Shakti.

अप्रबुद्धमतीनां हि एता बालविभीषिकाः ।
मातृमोदकवत्सर्वं प्रवृत्त्यर्थमुदाहृतम् ॥ १३ ॥
aprabuddhamatInAM hi EtA bAlavibhIShikAH 
mAtRmOdakavatsarvaM pravRttyarthamudAhRtam  13 

For those of unawakened understanding,
these are but frightening imaginings for the mind,
spoken only to induce engagement through example,
just as a mother entices a child with sweets to feed it.

दिक्कालकलनोन्मुक्ता देशोद्देशाविशेषिणी ।
व्यपदेष्टुमशक्यासावकथ्या परमार्थतः ॥ १४ ॥
dikkAlakalanOnmuktA dEshOddEshAvishEShiNI 
vyapadEShTumashakyAsAvakathyA paramArthataH  14 

Free from all calculations of direction and time,
without distinction of place or spatial designation,
that form cannot truly be pointed out,
and in the highest sense, cannot be even spoken of.

अन्तःस्वानुभवानन्दा विकल्पोन्मुक्तगोचरा ।
यावस्था भरिताकारा भैरवी भैरवात्मनः ॥ १५ ॥
antaHsvAnubhavAnandA vikalpOnmuktagOcharA 
yAvasthA bharitAkArA bhairavI bhairavAtmanaH  15 

That state of Bhairavī, belonging to the essence of Bhairava,
is the bliss of inner direct experience,
accessible only beyond conceptual thought,
and filled completely with fullness.

तद्वपुस्तत्त्वतो ज्ञेयं विमलं विश्वपूरणम् ।
एवंविधे परे तत्त्वे कः पूज्यः कश्च तृप्यति ॥ १६ ॥
tadvapustattvatO jnEyaM vimalaM vishvapUraNam 
EvaMvidhE parE tattvE kaH pUjyaH kashcha tRpyati  16 

That form should truly be known
as pure and all-pervading throughout creation.
In such a supreme Reality,
who is there to worship, and who can be satisfied?

एवंविधा भैरवस्य यावस्था परिगीयते ।
सा परा पररूपेण परा देवी प्रकीर्तिता ॥ १७ ॥
EvaMvidhA bhairavasya yAvasthA parigIyatE 
sA parA pararUpENa parA dEvI prakIrtitA  17 

Such a state of Bhairava as has been described
is called the Supreme Goddess,
who is supreme by being of the nature of the Formless.

शक्तिशक्तिमतोर्यद्वदभेदः सर्वदा स्थितः ।
अतस्तद्धर्मधर्मित्वात्परा शक्तिः परात्मनः ॥ १८ ॥
shaktishaktimatOryadvadabhEdaH sarvadA sthitaH 
atastaddharmadharmitvAtparA shaktiH parAtmanaH  18 

Just as power and the possessor of power
always remain non-different,
so too, because of the inseparability of attribute and bearer of attribute,
the Supreme Shakti belongs to the Supreme Self.

न वह्नेर्दाहिका शक्तिर्व्यतिरिक्ता विभाव्यते ।
केवलं ज्ञानसत्तायां प्रारम्भोऽयं प्रवेशने ॥ १९ ॥
na vahnErdAhikA shaktirvyatiriktA vibhAvyatE 
kEvalaM jnAnasattAyAM prArambhOayaM pravEshanE  19 

The burning power of fire
is never perceived as separate from the fire itself.
All this is taught merely
as an initial means of entering into the reality of knowledge.

शक्त्यवस्थाप्रविष्टस्य निर्विभागेन भावना ।
तदासौ शिवरूपी स्यात्शैवी मुखमिहोच्यते ॥ २० ॥
shaktyavasthApraviShTasya nirvibhAgEna bhAvanA 
tadAsau shivarUpI syAtshaivI mukhamihOchyatE  20 

For the one who has entered into the state of Shakti,
there arises contemplation free from all division.
Such a person then, becomes of the nature of Shiva.
This is said to be the entrance into the state of Shiva.

यथालोकेन दीपस्य किरणैर्भास्करस्य च ।
ज्ञायते दिग्विभागादि तद्वच्छक्त्या शिवः प्रिये ॥ २१ ॥
yathAlOkEna dIpasya kiraNairbhAskarasya cha 
jnAyatE digvibhAgAdi tadvachChaktyA shivaH priyE  21 

Just as, by the light of a lamp or by the rays of the sun,
directions and spatial distinctions become known,
so too, O Beloved, Shiva is known through Shakti.

श्री देव्युवाच ।
shrI dEvyuvAcha 

The auspicious Goddess spoke:

देवदेव त्रिशूलाङ्क कपालकृतभूषण ।
दिग्देशकालशून्या च व्यपदेशविवर्जिता ॥ २२ ॥
dEvadEva trishUlAnka kapAlakRtabhUShaNa 
digdEshakAlashUnyA cha vyapadEshavivarjitA  22 

O God of Gods, bearer of the trident,
adorned with garlands of skulls,
such a state which is beyond direction, place, and time,
and free from all designation,

यावस्था भरिताकारा भैरवस्योपलभ्यते ।
कैरुपायैर्मुखं तस्य परादेवी कथं भवेत् ।
यथा सम्यगहं वेद्मि तथा मे ब्रूहि भैरव ॥ २३ ॥
yAvasthA bharitAkArA bhairavasyOpalabhyatE 
kairupAyairmukhaM tasya parAdEvI kathaM bhavEt 
yathA samyagahaM vEdmi tathA mE brUhi bhairava  23 

that completely full state
through which Bhairava is realized,
through what means may one attain its entrance, O Deva?
Teach me, O Bhairava,
so that I may know this completely.

भैरव उवाच ।
bhairava uvAcha 

Bhairava spoke:

ऊर्ध्वे प्राणो ह्यधो जीवो विसर्गात्मा परोच्चरेत् ।
उत्पत्तिद्वितयस्थाने भरणाद्भरिता स्थितिः ॥ २४ ॥
UrdhvE prANO hyadhO jIvO visargAtmA parOchcharEt 
utpattidvitayasthAnE bharaNAdbharitA sthitiH  24 

The prāṇa moves upward, and the jīva moves downward.
By uttering the corresponding sounds externally,
and filling up one's focus on the origin of both these movements,
the state of fullness arises.

मरुतोऽन्तर्बहिर्वापि वियद्युग्मानिवर्तनात् ।
भैरव्या भैरवस्येत्थं भैरवि व्यज्यते वपुः ॥ २५ ॥
marutOantarbahirvApi viyadyugmAnivartanAt 
bhairavyA bhairavasyEtthaM bhairavi vyajyatE vapuH  25 

Whenever the breath moves outward or inward,
through restricting the duality of this movement,
O Bhairavī, the form of Bhairava is revealed.

न व्रजेन्न विशेच्छक्तिर्मरुद्रूपा विकासिते ।
निर्विकल्पतया मध्ये तया भैरवरूपता ॥ २६ ॥
na vrajEnna vishEchChaktirmarudrUpA vikAsitE 
nirvikalpatayA madhyE tayA bhairavarUpatA  26 

When the power in the form of breath
neither moves outward nor enters inward,
then, in the center, through that state free from conceptual thought,
the nature of Bhairava manifests.

कुम्भिता रेचिता वापि पूरिता वा यदा भवेत् ।
तदन्ते शान्तनामासौ शक्त्या शान्तः प्रकाशते ॥ २७ ॥
kumbhitA rEchitA vApi pUritA vA yadA bhavEt 
tadantE shAntanAmAsau shaktyA shAntaH prakAshatE  27 

Whether the breath is retained, exhaled, or inhaled,
at the end of that, through Shakti,
the peaceful one called Śānta shines forth.

आमूलात्किरणाभासां सूक्ष्मात्सूक्ष्मतरात्मिकम् ।
चिन्तयेत्तां द्विषट्कान्ते श्याम्यन्तीं भैरवोदयः ॥ २८ ॥
AmUlAtkiraNAbhAsAM sUkShmAtsUkShmatarAtmikam 
chintayEttAM dviShaTkAntE shyAmyantIM bhairavOdayaH  28 

One should meditate upon that subtle radiance,
finer than the finest, shining like rays
from the root upward, gradually dissolving at Dvādashānta.

उद्गच्छन्तीं तडिद्रूपां प्रतिचक्रं क्रमात्क्रमम् ।
ऊर्ध्वं मुष्टित्रयं यावत्तावदन्ते महोदयः ॥ २९ ॥
udgachChantIM taDidrUpAM pratichakraM kramAtkramam 
UrdhvaM muShTitrayaM yAvattAvadantE mahOdayaH  29 

One should contemplate upon the lightning-like energy
rising upward through each chakra,
step by step, up to the point three Mushtis above the head.
At its culmination, the great awakening arises.

क्रमद्वादशकं सम्यग्द्वादशाक्षरभेदितम् ।
स्थूलसूक्ष्मपरस्थित्या मुक्त्वा मुक्त्वान्ततः शिवः ॥ ३० ॥
kramadvAdashakaM samyagdvAdashAkSharabhEditam 
sthUlasUkShmaparasthityA muktvA muktvAntataH shivaH  30 

Passing properly through the sequence of the twelve,
pierced by the twelve syllables,
leaving behind the gross, the subtle, and the supreme states one after another,
in the end, one becomes Shiva.

तयापूर्याशु मूर्धान्तं भङ्क्त्वा भ्रूक्षेपसेतुना ।
निर्विकल्पं मनः कृत्वा सर्वोर्ध्वे सर्वगोद्गमः ॥ ३१ ॥
tayApUryAshu mUrdhAntaM bhanktvA bhrUkShEpasEtunA 
nirvikalpaM manaH kRtvA sarvOrdhvE sarvagOdgamaH  31 

Having filled the body up to the crown of the head
with the force of the breath, and crossing beyond
through the space between the eyebrows, if one makes the mind
completely free of thought, one rises beyond all limitations.

शिखिपक्षैश्चित्ररूपैर्मण्डलैः शून्यपञ्चकम् ।
ध्यायतोऽनुत्तरे शून्ये प्रवेशो हृदये भवेत् ॥ ३२ ॥
shikhipakShaishchitrarUpairmaNdalaiH shUnyapanchakam 
dhyAyatOanuttarE shUnyE pravEshO hRdayE bhavEt  32 

Meditating on the five inner spaces
through the many-colored patterns of sensory experience,
like the feathers of a peacock,
one enters the highest inner void within the heart.

ईदृशेन क्रमेणैव यत्र कुत्रापि चिन्तना ।
शून्ये कुड्ये परे पात्रे स्वयं लीना वरप्रदा ॥ ३३ ॥
IdRshEna kramENaiva yatra kutrApi chintanA 
shUnyE kuDyE parE pAtrE svayaM lInA varapradA  33 

Wherever attention settles through this method,
whether on empty space, a wall, or another person,
the mind naturally dissolves into it, revealing pure awareness.

कपालान्तर्मनो न्यस्य तिष्ठन्मीलितलोचनः ।
क्रमेण मनसो दार्ढ्यात् लक्षयेल्लक्ष्यमुत्तमम् ॥ ३४ ॥
kapAlAntarmanO nyasya tiShThanmIlitalOchanaH 
kramENa manasO dArDhyAtlakShayEtlaShyamuttamam  34 

Placing the mind within the inner space of the skull,
sitting still with closed eyes, and gradually stabilizing the mind,
one perceives the supreme reality.

मध्यनाडी मध्यसंस्था बिससूत्राभरूपया ।
ध्यातान्तर्व्योमया देव्या तया देवः प्रकाशते ॥ ३५ ॥
madhyanADI madhyasaMsthA bisasUtrAbharUpayA 
dhyAtAntarvyOmayA dEvyA tayA dEvaH prakAshatE  35 

The central channel exists between the breaths,
subtle like a lotus fiber. Meditating on it as the Goddess
who is the inner space, the Divine reveals itself through her.

कररुद्धदृगस्त्रेण भ्रूभेदाद्द्वाररोधनात् ।
दृष्टे बिन्दौ क्रमाल्लीने तन्मध्ये परमा स्थितिः ॥ ३६ ॥
kararuddhadRgastrENa bhrUbhEdAddvArarOdhanAt 
dRShTE bindau kramAllInE tanmadhyE paramA sthitiH  36 

By closing the senses with the hands,
focusing between the eyebrows, and perceiving
the inner point of light as it gradually dissolves,
one attains the supreme state at the center of consciousness.

धामान्तःक्षोभसम्भूतसूक्ष्माग्नितिलकाकृतिम् ।
बिन्दुं शिखान्ते हृदये लयान्ते ध्यायतो लयः ॥ ३७ ॥
dhAmAntaHkShObhasambhUtasUkShmAgnitilakAkRtim 
binduM shikhAntE hRdayE layAntE dhyAyatO layaH  37 

Meditate on the subtle point of light
within the crown and the heart,
like a tiny flame arising from inner intensity.
By doing so, the mind dissolves into stillness.

अनाहते पात्रकर्णेऽभग्नशब्दे सरिद्द्रुते ।
शब्दब्रह्मणि निष्णातः परं ब्रह्माधिगच्छति ॥ ३८ ॥
anAhatE pAtrakarNEabhagnashabdE sariddrutE 
shabdabrahmaNi niShNAtaH paraM brahmAdhigachChati  38 

One who becomes deeply absorbed
in the unstruck inner sound heard within the ear,
like the continuous roar of a flowing river,
attains the supreme reality.

प्रणवादिसमुच्चारात्प्लुतान्ते शून्यभावानात् ।
शून्यया परया शक्त्या शून्यतामेति भैरवि ॥ ३९ ॥
praNavAdisamuchchArAtplutAntE shUnyabhAvAnAt 
shUnyayA parayA shaktyA shUnyatAmEti bhairavi  39 

O Bhairavī, by chanting Pranava(Om) and meditating
on the silence at the end of its prolonged sound,
one enters the supreme emptiness
through the power of the Void itself.

यस्य कस्यापि वर्णस्य पूर्वान्तावनुभावयेत् ।
शून्यया शून्यभूतोऽसौ शून्याकारः पुमान् भवेत् ॥ ४० ॥
yasya kasyApi varNasya pUrvAntAvanubhAvayEt 
shUnyayA shUnyabhUtOasau shUnyAkAraH pumAn bhavEt  40 

One should become aware of both the beginning
and ending of any sound as emptiness.
By merging into that emptiness,
one’s very being becomes empty and boundless.

तन्त्र्यादिवाद्यशब्देषु दीर्घेषु क्रमसंस्थितेः ।
अनन्यचेताः प्रत्यन्ते परव्योमवपुर्भवेत् ॥ ४१ ॥
tantryAdivAdyashabdEShu dIrghEShu kramasaMsthitEH 
ananyachEtAH pratyantE paravyOmavapurbhavEt  41 

Listening with complete attention to the fading sounds
of musical instruments, especially as the sound comes to an end,
one realizes one’s own nature as the supreme inner space.

पिण्डमन्त्रस्य सर्वस्य स्थूलवर्णक्रमेण तु ।
अर्धेन्दुबिन्दुनादान्तः शून्योच्चाराद्भवेच्छिवः ॥ ४२ ॥
pindamantrasya sarvasya sthUlavarNakramENa tu 
ardhEndubindunAdAntaH shUnyOchchArAdbhavEchChivaH  42 

By inwardly following the subtle stages of mantra, 
from sound to silence, one ultimately becomes Śiva.

निजदेहे सर्वदिक्कं युगपद्भावयेद्वियत् ।
निर्विकल्पमनास्तस्य वियत्सर्वं प्रवर्तते ॥ ४३ ॥
nijadEhE sarvadikkaM yugapadbhAvayEdviyat 
nirvikalpamanAstasya viyatsarvaM pravartatE  43 

One should simultaneously feel the vastness of space
in all directions within one’s own body.
When the mind becomes free of thought,
everything is experienced as spacious and open.

पृष्ठशून्यं मूलशून्यं युगपद्भावयेच्च यः ।
शरीरनिरपेक्षिण्या शक्त्या शून्यमना भवेत् ॥ ४४ ॥
pRShThashUnyaM mUlashUnyaM yugapadbhAvayEchcha yaH 
sharIranirapEkShiNyA shaktyA shUnyamanA bhavEt  44 

One who simultaneously meditates
on the emptiness above and below the body
becomes free of mental activity through the power of pure consciousness.

पृष्ठशून्यं मूलशून्यं हृच्छून्यं भावयेत्स्थिरम् ।
युगपन्निर्विकल्पत्वान्निर्विकल्पोदयस्ततः ॥ ४५ ॥
pRShThashUnyaM mUlashUnyaM hRchChUnyaM bhAvayEtsthiram 
yugapannirvikalpatvAnnirvikalpOdayastataH  45 

Meditating steadily on the emptiness above,
below, and within the heart, while remaining free of thought,
leads to the arising of the thought-free state itself.

तनूदेशे शून्यतैव क्षणमात्रं विभावयेत् ।
निर्विकल्पं निर्विकल्पो निर्विकल्पस्वरूपभाक् ॥ ४६ ॥
tanUdEshE shUnyataiva kShaNamAtraM vibhAvayEt 
nirvikalpaM nirvikalpO nirvikalpasvarUpabhAk  46 

Even for a moment, if one deeply experiences emptiness
within any part of the body without thought,
one begins to share the nature of that pure thought-free reality.

सर्वं देहगतं द्रव्यं वियद्व्याप्तं मृगेक्षणे ।
विभावयेत्ततस्तस्य भावना सा स्थिरा भवेत् ॥ ४७ ॥
sarvaM dEhagataM dravyaM viyadvyAptaM mRgEkShaNE 
vibhAvayEttatastasya bhAvanA sA sthirA bhavEt  47 

O doe-eyed One, one should contemplate
that every substance within the body is pervaded by space.
Through this, meditation on emptiness becomes stable.

देहान्तरे त्वग्विभागं भित्तिभूतं विचिन्तयेत् ।
न किंचिदन्तरे तस्य ध्यायन्नध्येयभाग्भवेत् ॥ ४८ ॥
dEhAntarE tvagvibhAgaM bhittibhUtaM vichintayEt 
na kiMchidantarE tasya dhyAyannadhyEyabhAgbhavEt  48 

One should regard the skin as merely a wall,
with nothing truly existing inside it.
Meditating in this way, one enters that
which cannot be grasped as an object.

हृद्याकाशे निलीनाक्षः पद्मसम्पुटमध्यगः ।
अनन्यचेताः सुभगे परं सौभाग्यमाप्नुयात् ॥ ४९ ॥
hRdyAkAshE nilInAkShaH padmasampuTamadhyagaH 
ananyachEtAH subhagE paraM saubhAgyamApnuyAt  49 

O blessed one, when the senses dissolve
into the inner space of the heart and the mind rests
undistracted within the lotus of the heart,
supreme fortune is attained.

सर्वतः स्वशरीरस्य द्वादशान्ते मनोलयात् ।
दृढबुद्धेर्दृढीभूतं तत्त्वलक्ष्यं प्रवर्तते ॥ ५० ॥
sarvataH svasharIrasya dvAdashAntE manOlayAt 
dRDhabuddhErdRDhIbhUtaM tattvalakShyaM pravartatE  50 

By dissolving the mind into the subtle space
at the limits of the body in every direction,
the ultimate reality becomes firmly established in awareness.

यथा तथा यत्र तत्र द्वादशान्ते मनः क्षिपेत् ॥
प्रतिक्षणं क्षीणवृत्तेर्वैलक्षण्यं दिनैर्भवेत् ॥ ५१ ॥
yathA tathA yatra tatra dvAdashAntE manaH kShipEt 
pratikShaNaM kShINavRttErvailakShaNyaM dinairbhavEt  51 

One should repeatedly cast the mind into the subtle
inner space wherever one may be.
As mental activity weakens through practice,
extraordinary awareness arises within days.

कालाग्निना कालपदादुत्थितेन स्वकं पुरम् ।
प्लुष्टं विचिन्तयेदन्ते शान्ताभासस्तदा भवेत् ॥ ५२ ॥
kAlAgninA kAlapadAdutthitEna svakaM puram 
pluShTaM vichintayEdantE shAntAbhAsastadA bhavEt  52 

One should imagine the body being burned to ashes
by the fire of time rising upward from the feet.
In the end, a profound peacefulness manifests.

एवमेव जगत्सर्वं दग्धं ध्यात्वा विकल्पतः ।
अनन्यचेतसः पुंसः पुम्भावः परमो भवेत् ॥ ५३ ॥
EvamEva jagatsarvaM dagdhaM dhyAtvA vikalpataH 
ananyachEtasaH puMsaH pumbhAvaH paramO bhavEt  53 

In the same way, imagining the entire creation
being consumed by fire, and remaining fully focused,
one attains the supreme state of being.

स्वदेहे जगतो वापि सूक्ष्मसूक्ष्मतराणि च ।
तत्त्वानि यानि निलयं ध्यात्वान्ते व्यज्यते परा ॥ ५४ ॥
svadEhE jagatO vApi sUkShmasUkShmatarANi cha 
tattvAni yAni nilayaM dhyAtvAntE vyajyatE parA  54 

Meditating on the dissolution of all subtle principles
within the body or the creation into one another,
the supreme Goddess Parā reveals herself.

पीनां च दुर्बलां शक्तिं ध्यात्वा द्वादशगोचरे ।
प्रविश्य हृदये ध्यायन्मुक्तः स्वातन्त्र्यमाप्नुयात् ॥ ५५ ॥
pInAM cha durbalAM shaktiM dhyAtvA dvAdashagOcharE 
pravishya hRdayE dhyAyanmuktaH svAtantryamApnuyAt  55 

Meditating on the breath as both strong and subtle
in the region beyond the body, then entering the heart in sleep,
one gains mastery over dreams.

भुवनाध्वादिरूपेण चिन्तयेत्क्रमशोऽखिलम् ।
स्थूलसूक्ष्मपरस्थित्या यावदन्ते मनोलयः ॥ ५६ ॥
bhuvanAdhvAdirUpENa chintayEtkramashOakhilam 
sthUlasUkShmaparasthityA yAvadantE manOlayaH  56 

One should contemplate the entire creation step by step,
in its gross, subtle, and supreme forms,
until the mind dissolves completely.

अस्य सर्वस्य विश्वस्य पर्यन्तेषु समन्ततः ।
अध्वप्रक्रियया तत्त्वं शैवं ध्यत्वा महोदयः ॥ ५७ ॥
asya sarvasya vishvasya paryantEShu samantataH 
adhvaprakriyayA tattvaM shaivaM dhyatvA mahOdayaH  57 

By meditating on the entire creation through all levels
and dimensions as expressions of Śiva, a great awakening arises.

विश्वमेतन्महादेवि शून्यभूतं विचिन्तयेत् ।
तत्रैव च मनो लीनं ततस्तल्लयभाजनम् ॥ ५८ ॥
vishvamEtanmahAdEvi shUnyabhUtaM vichintayEt 
tatraiva cha manO lInaM tatastallayabhAjanam  58 

O Great Goddess, one should contemplate the whole universe
as emptiness, and dissolve the mind into that very emptiness.
Then one becomes fit to merge into it completely.

घटादिभाजने दृष्टिं भित्तीस्त्यक्त्वा विनिक्षिपेत् ।
तल्लयं तत्क्षणाद्गत्वा तल्लयात्तन्मयो भवेत् ॥ ५९ ॥
ghatAdibhAjanE dRShTiM bhittIstyaktvA vinikShipEt 
tallayaM tatkShaNAdgatvA tallayAttanmayO bhavEt  59 

Gazing into the empty space inside a pot or vessel,
ignoring its outer walls, one instantly dissolves into that emptiness
and becomes one with it.

निर्वृक्षगिरिभित्त्यादिदेशे दृष्टिं विनिक्षिपेत् ।
विलीने मानसे भावे वृत्तिक्षीणः प्रजायते ॥ ६० ॥
nirvRkShagiribhittyAdidEshE dRShTiM vinikShipEt 
vilInE mAnasE bhAvE vRttikShiNaH prajAyatE  60 

Gazing into an open landscape free from trees,
mountains, walls, or distractions, the mind dissolves
and becomes free from restless thoughts.

उभयोर्भावयोर्ज्ञाने ध्यात्वा मध्यं समाश्रयेत् ।
युगपच्च द्वयं त्यक्त्वा मध्ये तत्त्वं प्रकाशते ॥ ६१ ॥
ubhayOrbhAvayOrjnAnE dhyAtvA madhyaM samAshrayEt 
yugapachcha dvayaM tyaktvA madhyE tattvaM prakAshatE  61 

When aware of two different things or states,
rest in the center between them. Abandoning both together,
reality shines forth in the middle.

भावे न्यक्ते निरुद्धा चिन्नैव भावान्तरं व्रजेत् ।
तदा तन्मध्यभावेन विकसत्यति भावना ॥ ६२ ॥
bhAvE nyaktE niruddhA chinnaiva bhAvAntaraM vrajEt 
tadA tanmadhyabhAvEna vikasatyati bhAvanA  62 

Once one thought has been dropped
and the mind remains still without moving to another thought,
a higher state of meditation blossoms from that silent center.

सर्वं देहं चिन्मयं हि जगद्वा परिभावयेत् ।
युगपन्निर्विकल्पेन मनसा परमोदयः ॥ ६३ ॥
sarvaM dEhaM chinmayaM hi jagadvA paribhAvayEt 
yugapannirvikalpEna manasA paramOdayaH  63 

One should contemplate the whole body
or the entire universe as nothing but consciousness.
Through a thought-free mind, supreme awakening arises.

वायुद्वयस्य संघट्टादन्तर्वा बहिरन्ततः ।
योगी समत्वविज्ञानसमुद्गमनभाजनम् ॥ ६४ ॥
vAyudvayasya saMghaTTAdantarvA bahirantataH 
yOgI samatvavijnAnasamudgamanabhAjanam  64 

When the two breaths meet, either within or outside,
and finally come to rest, the yogi experiences the knowledge
of the underlying equality of all things.

सर्वं जगत्स्वदेहं वा स्वानन्दभरितं स्मरेत् ।
युगपत्स्वामृतेनैव परानन्दमयो भवेत् ॥ ६५ ॥
sarvaM jagatsvadEhaM vA svAnandabharitaM smarEt 
yugapatsvAmRtEnaiva parAnandamayO bhavEt  65 

One should remember that the whole universe
or one’s own body is filled with innate bliss.
Through that nectar of inner bliss, one becomes supreme bliss itself.

कुहनेन प्रयोगेन सद्य एव मृगेक्षणे ।
समुदेति महानन्दो येन तत्त्वं प्रकाशते ॥ ६६ ॥
kuhanEna prayOgEna sadya Eva mRgEkShaNE 
samudEti mahAnandO yEna tattvaM prakAshatE  66 

O doe-eyed One, through certain sudden bodily sensations,
intense bliss can unexpectedly arise,
through which the ultimate reality becomes revealed.

सर्वस्रोतोनिबन्धेन प्राणशक्त्योर्ध्वया शनैः ।
पिपीलस्पर्शवेलायां प्रथते परमं सुखम् ॥ ६७ ॥
sarvasrOtOnibandhEna prANashaktyOrdhvayA shanaiH 
pipIlasparshavElAyAM prathatE paramaM sukham  67 

When the life-force rises upward and produces
sensations like ants crawling on the body, supreme bliss unfolds.

वह्नेर्विषस्य मध्ये तु चित्तं सुखमयं क्षिपेत् ।
केवलं वायुपूर्णं वा स्मरानन्देन युज्यते ॥ ६८ ॥
vahnErviShasya madhyE tu chittaM sukhamayaM kShipEt 
kEvalaM vAyupUrNaM vA smarAnandEna yujyatE  68 

During lovemaking, one should place the mind
in the blissful state between the rising of desire and its final release,
or fill it only with breath-awareness.
In this way, consciousness merges with bliss.

शक्तिसंगमसंक्षुब्धशक्त्यावेशावसानिकम् ।
यत्सुखं ब्रह्मतत्त्वस्य तत्सुखं स्वाक्यमुच्यते ॥ ६९ ॥
shaktisaMgamasaMkShubdhashaktyAvEshAvasAnikam 
yatsukhaM brahmatattvasya tatsukhaM svAkyamuchyatE  69 

The bliss experienced at the climax of union with one’s partner
is declared to be the bliss of Brahman itself: one’s own deepest nature.

लेहनामन्थनाकोटैः स्त्रीसुखस्य भरात्स्मृतेः ।
शक्त्यभावेऽपि देवेशि भवेदानन्दसम्प्लवः ॥ ७० ॥
lEhanAmanthanAkOTaiH strIsukhasya bharAtsmRtEH 
shaktyabhAvEapi dEvEshi bhavEdAnandasamplavaH  70 

O Goddess of gods, even without a physical partner,
remembering the intensity pleasure, arising from kissing,
embracing, and union, can generate a flood of bliss.

आनन्दे महति प्राप्ते दृष्टे वा बान्धवे चिरात् ।
आनन्दमुद्गतं ध्यात्वा तल्लयस्तन्मना भवेत् ॥ ७१ ॥
AnandE mahati prAptE dRShTE vA bAndhavE chirAt 
AnandamudgataM dhyAtvA tallayastanmanA bhavEt  71 

When great joy arises, such as during love
or upon meeting a dear one after a long separation, 
meditate upon that very bliss as it emerges.
The mind then dissolves into it.

जग्धिपानकृतोल्लासरसानन्दविजृम्भणात् ।
भावयेद्भरितावस्थां महानन्दस्ततो भवेत् ॥ ७२ ॥
jagdhipAnakRtOllAsarasAnandavijRmbhaNAt 
bhAvayEdbharitAvasthAM mahAnandastatO bhavEt  72 

One should contemplate the fullness of joy
that arises from eating and drinking with deep awareness.
Through this, great bliss appears.

गीतादिविषयास्वादासमसौख्यैकतात्मनः ।
योगिनस्तन्मयत्वेन मनोरूढेस्तदात्मता ॥ ७३ ॥
gitAdiviShayAsvAdAsamasaukhyaikatAtmanaH 
yOginastanmayatvEna manOrUDhEstadAtmatA  73 

A yogi who becomes fully absorbed in the joy
of music and other beautiful sensory experiences
becomes one with that bliss itself.

यत्र यत्र मनस्तुष्टिर्मनस्तत्रैव धारयेत् ।
तत्र तत्र परानन्दस्वारूपं सम्प्रवर्तते ॥ ७४ ॥
yatra yatra manastuShTirmanastatraiva dhArayEt 
tatra tatra parAnandasvArUpaM sampravartatE  74 

Wherever the mind naturally feels contentment,
one should hold attention there.
In that very place, supreme bliss manifests.

अनागतायां निद्रायां प्रणष्टे बाह्यगोचरे ।
सावस्था मनसा गम्या परा देवी प्रकाशते ॥ ७५ ॥
anAgatAyAM nidrAyAM praNaShTE bAhyagOcharE 
sAvasthA manasA gamyA parA dEvI prakAshatE  75 

The subtle state experienced just before sleep,
when outer awareness has faded but sleep has not yet begun,
is the revelation of the supreme Goddess.

तेजसा सूर्यदीपादेराकाशे शबलीकृते ।
दृष्टिर्निवेश्या तत्रैव स्वात्मरूपं प्रकाशते ॥ ७६ ॥
tEjasA sUryadIpAdErAkAshE shabalIkRtE 
dRShTirnivEshyA tatraiva svAtmarUpaM prakAshatE  76 

Gazing steadily into space illuminated
by sunlight or a lamp, one’s own true nature
becomes revealed there.

करङ्किण्या क्रोधनया भैरव्या लेलिहानया ।
खेचर्या दृष्टिकाले च परावाप्तिः प्रकाशते ॥ ७७ ॥
karankiNyA krOdhanayA bhairavyA lElihAnayA 
khEcharyA dRShTikAlE cha parAvAptiH prakAshatE  77 

Through certain yogic mudrās such as Karaṅkinī,
Krodhanā, Bhairavī, Lelihānā, and Khecarī,
the supreme expansion of consciousness is revealed.

मृद्वासने स्फिजैकेन हस्तपादौ निराश्रयम् ।
निधाय तत्प्रसङ्गेन परा पूर्णा मतिर्भवेत् ॥ ७८ ॥
mRdvAsanE sphijaikEna hastapAdau nirAshrayam 
nidhAya tatprasangEna parA pUrNA matirbhavEt  78 

Sitting on a soft seat balanced on one buttock,
with hands and feet unsupported,
the mind becomes filled with supreme awareness.

उपविश्यासने सम्यग्बाहू कृत्वार्धकुञ्चितौ ।
कक्षव्योम्नि मनः कुर्वन् शममायाति तल्लयात् ॥ ७९ ॥
upavishyAsanE samyagbAhU kRtvArdhakunchitau 
kakShavyOmni manaH kurvan shamamAyAti tallayAt  79 

Sitting with the arms slightly bent
and the mind focused in the space beneath the armpits,
the yogi becomes peaceful through absorption.

स्थूलरूपस्य भावस्य स्तब्धां दृष्टिं निपात्य च ।
अचिरेण निराधारं मनः कृत्वा शिवं व्रजेत् ॥ ८० ॥
sthUlarUpasya bhAvasya stabdhAM dRShTiM nipAtya cha 
achirENa nirAdhAraM manaH kRtvA shivaM vrajEt  80 

Fixing the gaze steadily upon a beautiful physical form
while freeing the mind from dependence on thought,
one quickly attains tranquility.

मध्यजिह्वे स्फारितास्ये मध्ये निक्षिप्य चेतनाम् ।
होच्चारं मनसा कुर्वंस्ततः शान्ते प्रलीयते ॥ ८१ ॥
madhyajihvE sphAritAsyE madhyE nikShipya chEtanAm 
hOchchAraM manasA kurvaMstataH shAntE pralIyatE  81 

Turning the tongue upward, opening the mouth slightly,
and fixing awareness in the center while mentally sounding “Ha,”
one dissolves into deep peace.

आसने शयने स्थित्वा निराधारं विभावयन् ।
स्वदेहं मनसि क्षीणे क्षणात्क्षीणाशयो भवेत् ॥ ८२ ॥
AsanE shayanE sthitvA nirAdhAraM vibhAvayan 
svadEhaM manasi kShiNE kShaNAtkShINAshayO bhavEt  82 

Whether sitting or lying down,
meditating on the body as unsupported and groundless,
when the mind becomes still, all inner conditioning dissolves instantly.

चलासने स्थितस्याथ शनैर्वा देहचालनात् ।
प्रशान्ते मानसे भावे देवि दिव्यौघमाप्नुयात् ॥ ८३ ॥
chalAsanE sthitasyAtha shanairvA dEhachAlanAt 
prashAntE mAnasE bhAvE dEvi divyaughamApnuyAt  83 

O Goddess, whether seated in a moving vehicle
or gently moving the body oneself,
when the mind becomes peaceful, divine consciousness floods in.

आकाशं विमलं पश्यन् कृत्वा दृष्टिं निरन्तराम् ।
स्तब्धात्मा तत्क्षणाद्देवि भैरवं वपुराप्नुयात् ॥ ८४ ॥
AkAshaM vimalaM pashyan kRtvA dRShTiM nirantarAm 
stabdhAtmA tatkShaNAddEvi bhairavaM vapurApnuyAt  84 

Gazing steadily into the clear sky without blinking,
remaining completely still,
one instantly attains the nature of Bhairava.

लीनं मूर्ध्नि वियत्सर्वं भैरवत्वेन भावयेत् ।
तत्सर्वं भैरवाकारतेजस्तत्त्वं समाविशेत् ॥ ८५ ॥
lInaM mUrdhni viyatsarvaM bhairavatvEna bhAvayEt 
tatsarvaM bhairavAkAratEjastattvaM samAvishEt  85 

One should imagine the entire sky merged
within the head as Bhairava.
Then everything becomes absorbed
into the radiant energy of Bhairava’s being.

किञ्चिज्ज्ञातं द्वैतदायि बाह्यालोकस्तमः पुनः ।
विश्वादि भैरवं रूपं ज्ञात्वानन्तप्रकाशभृत् ॥ ८६ ॥
kinchijjnAtaM dvaitadAyi bAhyAlOkastamaH punaH 
vishvAdi bhairavaM rUpaM jnAtvAnantaprakAshabhRt  86 

Realizing Bhairava as the underlying reality of waking,
dreaming, and deep sleep,
one becomes filled with infinite light.

एवमेव दुर्निशायां कृष्णपक्षागमे चिरम् ।
तैमिरं भावयन् रूपं भैरवं रूपमेष्यति ॥ ८७ ॥
EvamEva durnishAyAM kRShNapakShAgamE chiram 
taimiraM bhAvayan rUpaM bhairavaM rUpamEShyati  87 

Similarly, meditating for a long time
on deep darkness during a moonless night,
one attains the nature of Bhairava.

एवमेव निमील्यादौ नेत्रे कृष्णाभमग्रतः ।
प्रसार्य भैरवं रूपं भावयंस्तन्मयो भवेत् ॥ ८८ ॥
EvamEva nimIlyAdau nEtrE kRShNAbhamagrataH 
prasArya bhairavaM rUpaM bhAvayaMstanmayO bhavEt  88 

Closing the eyes and meditating on darkness,
then opening them while maintaining awareness
of that same darkness, one becomes one with Bhairava.

यस्य कस्येन्द्रियस्यापि व्याघाताच्च निरोधतः ।
प्रविष्टस्याद्वये शून्ये तत्रैवात्मा प्रकाशते ॥ ८९ ॥
yasya kasyEndriyasyApi vyAghAtAchcha nirOdhataH 
praviShTasyAdvayE shUnyE tatraivAtmA prakAshatE  89 

By suddenly interrupting or restraining
any sense perception, the true Self shines forth
within the non-dual void.

अबिन्दुमविसर्गं च अकारं जपतो महान् ।
उदेति देवि सहसा ज्ञानौघः परमेश्वरः ॥ ९० ॥
abindumavisargaM cha akAraM japatO mahAn 
udEti dEvi sahasA jnAnaughaH paramEshvaraH  90 

O Goddess, by repeating the sound “a”
without any additional vibration or ending,
a great flood of divine knowledge arises suddenly.

वर्णस्य सविसर्गस्य विसर्गान्तं चितिं कुरु ।
निराधारेण चित्तेन स्पृशेद्ब्रह्म सनातनम् ॥ ९१ ॥
varNasya savisargasya visargAntaM chitiM kuru 
nirAdhArENa chittEna spRshEdbrahma sanAtanam  91 

Fix awareness on the ending of a sound
that ends in visarga, and through a mind free of dependence,
touch the eternal Brahman.

व्योमाकारं स्वमात्मानं ध्यायेद्दिग्भिरनावृतम् ।
निराश्रया चितिः शक्तिः स्वरूपं दर्शयेत्तदा ॥ ९२ ॥
vyOmAkAraM svamAtmAnaM dhyAyEddigbhiranAvRtam 
nirAshrayA chitiH shaktiH svarUpaM darshayEttadA  92 

Meditating on one’s Self as vast like the sky
and unrestricted in all directions,
the power of consciousness reveals one’s true nature.

किंचिदङ्गं विभिद्यादौ तीक्ष्णसूच्यादिना ततः ।
तत्रैव चेतनां युक्त्वा भैरवे निर्मला गतिः ॥ ९३ ॥
kiMchidangaM vibhidyAdau tIkShNasUchyAdinA tataH 
tatraiva chEtanAM yuktvA bhairavE nirmalA gatiH  93 

Piercing any part of the body lightly
with a sharp object and focusing awareness there,
one attains the pure state of Bhairava.

चित्ताद्यन्तःकृतिर्नास्ति ममान्तर्भावयेदिति ।
विकल्पानामभावेन विकल्पैरुज्झितो भवेत् ॥ ९४ ॥
chittAdyantaHkRtirnAsti mamAntarbhAvayEditi 
vikalpAnAmabhAvEna vikalpairujjhitO bhavEt  94 

One should reflect: “Within me there is no mind, intellect, or ego.”
Through freedom from thought constructs,
one becomes free of them altogether.

माया विमोहिनी नाम कलायाः कलनं स्थितम् ।
इत्यादिधर्मं तत्त्वानां कलयन्न पृथग्भवेत् ॥ ९५ ॥
mAyA vimOhinI nAma kalAyAH kalanaM sthitam 
ityAdidharmaM tattvAnAM kalayanna pRthagbhavEt  95 

Understanding the qualities of the cosmic principles,
such as Māyā causing delusion, limitation, and division,
one no longer experiences separation from Śiva.

झगितीच्छां समुत्पन्नामवलोक्य शमं नयेत् ।
यत एव समुद्भूता ततस्तत्रैव लीयते ॥ ९६ ॥
jhagitIchChAM samutpannAmavalOkya shamaM nayEt 
yata Eva samudbhUtA tatastatraiva lIyatE  96 

The moment a desire arises, one should observe it
and immediately let it dissolve.
It then returns to the very source from which it emerged.

यदा ममेच्छा नोत्पन्ना ज्ञानं वा कस्तदास्मि वै ।
तत्त्वतोऽहम् तथाभूतस्तल्लीनस्तन्मना भवेत् ॥ ९७ ॥
yadA mamEchChA nOtpannA jnAnaM vA kastadAsmi vai 
tattvatOhaM tathAbhUtastallInastanmanA bhavEt  97 

“Who am I when neither desire nor knowledge has arisen?”
Reflecting in this way, one merges into one’s true nature.

इच्छायामथवा ज्ञाने जाते चित्तं निवेशयेत् ।
आत्मबुद्ध्यानन्यचेतास्ततस्तत्त्वार्थदर्शनम् ॥ ९८ ॥
ichChAyAmathavA jnAnE jAtE chittaM nivEshayEt 
AtmabuddhyAnanyachEtAstatastattvArthadarshanam  98 

When desire or knowledge arises,
focus the mind on the point from which it emerges.
Remaining centered in awareness of the Self,
one perceives reality directly.

निर्निमित्तं भवेज्ज्ञानं निराधारं भ्रमात्मकम् ।
तत्त्वतः कस्यचिन्नैतदेवम्भावी शिवः प्रिये ॥ ९९ ॥
nirnimittaM bhavEjjnAnaM nirAdhAraM bhramAtmakam 
tattvataH kasyachinnaitadEvambhAvI shivaH priyE  99 

All ordinary knowledge is baseless and deceptive.
In truth, it belongs to no individual.
One who lives in this understanding is Śiva.

चिद्धर्मा सर्वदेहेषु विशेषो नास्ति कुत्रचित् ।
अतश्च तन्मयं सर्वं भावयन् भवजिज्जनः ॥ १०० ॥
chiddharmA sarvadEhEShu vishEShO nAsti kutrachit 
atashcha tanmayaM sarvaM bhAvayan bhavajijjanaH  100 

Consciousness exists equally in all beings without distinction.
Meditating on everything as expressions of consciousness,
one transcends worldly existence.

कामक्रोधलोभमोहमदमात्सर्यगोचरे ।
बुद्धिं निस्तिमितां कृत्वा तत्तत्त्वमवशिष्यते ॥ १०१ ॥
kAmakrOdhalObhamOhamadamAtsaryagOcharE 
buddhiM nistimitAM kRtvA tattattvamavashiShyatE  101 

When the mind becomes completely still
in moments of desire, anger, greed, confusion, pride,
or jealousy, the underlying reality reveals itself.

इन्द्रजालमयं विश्वं व्यस्तं वा चित्रकर्मवत् ।
भ्रमद्वा ध्यायतः सर्वं पश्यतश्च सुखोद्गमः ॥ १०२ ॥
indrajAlamayaM vishvaM vyastaM vA chitrakarmavat 
bhramadvA dhyAyataH sarvaM pashyatashcha sukhOdgamaH  102 

Seeing the universe as a magical illusion,
a painted image, or something constantly shifting,
one experiences the arising of bliss.

न चित्तं निक्षिपेद्दुःखे न सुखे वा परिक्षिपेत् ।
भैरवि ज्ञायतां मध्ये किं तत्त्वमवशिष्यते ॥ १०३ ॥
na chittaM nikShipEdduHkhE na sukhE vA parikShipEt 
bhairavi jnAyatAM madhyE kiM tattvamavashiShyatE  103 

Do not throw the mind into pleasure or suffering.
O Bhairavī, discover the reality that exists in the middle
between them.

विहाय निजदेहस्थं सर्वत्रास्मीति भावयन् ।
दृढेन मनसा दृष्ट्या नान्येक्षिण्या सुखी भवेत् ॥ १०४ ॥
vihAya nijadEhasthaM sarvatrAsmIti bhAvayan 
dRDhEna manasA dRShTyA nAnyEkShiNyA sukhI bhavEt  104 

Letting go of attachment to one’s body
and deeply feeling “I am everywhere,”
with steady awareness, one becomes blissful.

घटादौ यच्च विज्ञानमिच्छाद्यं वा ममान्तरे ।
नैव सर्वगतं जातं भावयन्निति सर्वगः ॥ १०५ ॥
ghaTAdau yachcha vijnAnamichChAdyaM vA mamAntarE 
naiva sarvagataM jAtaM bhAvayaniti sarvagaH  105 

One should contemplate: “Consciousness and will
are not only within me; they are present in all things.”
Through this, one becomes all-pervading.

ग्राह्यग्राहकसंवित्तिः सामान्या सर्वदेहिनाम् ।
योगिनां तु विशेषोऽस्ति सम्बन्धे सावधानता ॥ १०६ ॥
grAhyagrAhakasaMvittiH sAmAnyA sarvadEhinAm 
yOginAM tu vishEShOsti sambandhE sAvadhAnatA  106 

Awareness of subject and object exists in everyone.
What distinguishes yogis is their deep awareness
of the relationship between them.

स्ववदन्यशरीरेऽपि संवित्तिमनुभावयेत् ।
अपेक्षां स्वशरीरस्य त्यक्त्वा व्यापी दिनैर्भवेत् ॥ १०७ ॥
svavadanyasharIrEpi saMvittimanubhAvayEt 
apEkShAM svasharIrasya tyaktvA vyApI dinairbhavEt  107 

One should experience consciousness in
another person’s body just as in one’s own.
Letting go of attachment to one’s own body,
one becomes expansive and all-pervading.

निराधारं मनः कृत्वा विकल्पान्न विकल्पयेत् ।
तदात्मपरमात्मत्वे भैरवो मृगलोचने ॥ १०८ ॥
nirAdhAraM manaH kRtvA vikalpAnna vikalpayEt 
tadAtmaparamAtmatvE bhairavO mRgalOchanE  108 

Making the mind free of support and ceasing all thoughts,
O beautiful-eyed one, the individual self is realized
as the Supreme Self: Bhairava himself.

सर्वज्ञः सर्वकर्ता च व्यापकः परमेश्वरः ।
स एवाहं शैवधर्मा इति दार्ढ्याच्छिवो भवेत् ॥ १०९ ॥
sarvajnaH sarvakartA cha vyApakaH paramEshvaraH 
sa EvAhaM shaivadharmA iti dArDhyAchChivO bhavEt  109 

The Supreme Lord is all-knowing, all-doing, and all-pervading.
Firmly realizing “I am that Śiva,” one becomes Śiva.

जलस्येवोर्मयो वह्नेर्ज्वालाभङ्ग्यः प्रभा रवेः ।
ममैव भैरवस्यैता विश्वभङ्ग्यो विभेदिताः ॥ ११० ॥
jalasyEvOrmayO vahnErjvAlAbhangyaH prabhA ravEH 
mamaiva bhairavasyaitA vishvabhangyO vibhEditAH  110 

Just as waves arise from water, flames from fire, and rays from the sun,
all the movements of the universe arise from me, Bhairava.

भ्रान्त्वा भ्रान्त्वा शरीरेण त्वरितं भुवि पातनात् ।
क्षोभशक्तिविरामेण परा संजायते दशा ॥ १११ ॥
bhrAntvA bhrAntvA sharIrENa tvaritaM bhuvi pAtanAt 
kShObhashaktivirAmENa parA saMjAyatE dashA  111 

Spinning the body rapidly and suddenly falling to the ground,
when the agitation stops, the supreme state arises.

आधारेष्वथवाऽशक्त्याऽज्ञानाच्चित्तलयेन वा ।
जातशक्तिसमावेशक्षोभान्ते भैरवं वपुः ॥ ११२ ॥
AdhArEShvathavAashaktyAajnAnAchchittalayEna vA 
jAtashaktisamAvEshakShObhAntE bhairavaM vapuH  112 

When the agitation caused by weakness,
sensory exhaustion, or mental collapse comes to an end,
Bhairava’s pure nature becomes revealed.

सम्प्रदायमिमं देवि शृणु सम्यग्वदाम्यहम् ।
कैवल्यं जायते सद्यो नेत्रयोः स्तब्धमात्रयोः ॥ ११३ ॥
sampradAyamimaM dEvi shRNu samyagvadAmyaham 
kaivalyaM jAyatE sadyO nEtrayOH stabdhamAtrayOH  113 

O Goddess, listen carefully to this sacred teaching:
by holding the eyes completely still without blinking,
liberation arises immediately.

संकोचं कर्णयोः कृत्वा ह्यधोद्वारे तथैव च ।
अनच्कमहलं ध्यायन् विशेद्ब्रह्म सनातनम् ॥ ११४ ॥
saMkOchaM karNayOH kRtvA hyadhOdvArE tathaiva cha 
anachkamahalaM dhyAyan vishEdbrahma sanAtanam  114 

Closing the ears and the lower opening of the body,
and meditating on the inner unstruck sound
beyond ordinary speech, one enters the eternal Brahman.

कूपादिके महागर्ते स्थित्वोपरि निरीक्षणात् ।
अविकल्पमतेः सम्यग्सद्यश्चित्तलयः स्फुटम् ॥ ११५ ॥
kUpAdikE mahAgartE sthitvOpari nirIkShaNAt 
avikalpamatEH samyaksadyaschittalayaH sphuTam  115 

Standing above a deep well or abyss and gazing into it
with a mind free of thought, the mind clearly dissolves away.

यत्र यत्र मनो याति बाह्ये वाभ्यन्तरेऽपि वा ।
तत्र तत्र शिवावास्था व्यापकत्वात्क्व यास्यति ॥ ११६ ॥
yatra yatra manO yAti bAhyE vAbhyantarEapi vA 
tatra tatra shivAvAsthA vyApakatvAtkva yAsyati  116 

Wherever the mind moves, within or without,
Śiva’s state exists there, for nothing exists outside
His all-pervading presence.

यत्र यत्राक्षमार्गेण चैतन्यं व्यज्यते विभोः ।
तस्य तन्मात्रधर्मित्वाच्चिल्लयाद्भरितात्मता ॥ ११७ ॥
yatra yatrAkShamArgENa chaitanyaM vyajyatE vibhOH 
tasya tanmAtradharmitvAchchillayAdbharitAtmatA  117 

Wherever universal consciousness reveals itself
through any sense, dissolving into that awareness
leads to fullness and completeness.

क्षुताद्यन्ते भये शोके गह्वरे वा रणाद्द्रुते ।
कुतूहलेक्षुधाद्यन्ते ब्रह्मसत्तामयी दशा ॥ ११८ ॥
kShutAdyantE bhayE shOkE gahvarE vA raNAddrutE 
kutUhalEkShudhAdyantE brahmasattAmayI dashA  118 

The state of Brahman is near at moments
such as the rise or end of anger, fear, grief,
intense curiosity, danger, or hunger.

वस्तुषु स्मर्यमाणेषु दृष्टे देशे मनस्त्यजेत् ।
स्वशरीरं निराधारं कृत्वा प्रसरति प्रभुः ॥ ११९ ॥
vastuShu smaryamANEShu dRShTE dEshE manastyajEt 
svasharIraM nirAdhAraM kRtvA prasarati prabhuH  119 

While looking at a place, letting go
of all remembered objects and making the body unsupported,
the Divine presence expands forth.

क्वचिद्वस्तुनि विन्यस्य शनैर्दृष्टिं निवर्तयेत् ।
तज्ज्ञानं चित्तसहितं देवि शून्यालायो भवेत् ॥१२० ॥
kvachidvastuni vinyasya shanairdRShTiM nivartayEt 
tajjnAnaM chittasahitaM dEvi shUnyAlAyO bhavEt 120 

Fixing the gaze upon an object and then slowly
withdrawing attention from it until both object
and thought disappear, one becomes an abode of emptiness.

भक्त्युद्रेकाद्विरक्तस्य यादृशी जायते मतिः ।
सा शक्तिः शाङ्करी नित्यं भावयेत्तां ततः शिवः ॥ १२१ ॥
bhaktyudrEkAdviraktasya yAdRshI jAyatE matiH 
sA shaktiH shAnkarI nityaM bhavayEttAM tataH shivaH  121 

The intuitive awareness that arises through intense devotion
and detachment is Śiva’s own power.
Constantly contemplate that power and become Śiva.

वस्त्वन्तरे वेद्यमाने सर्ववस्तुषु शून्यता ।
तामेव मनसा ध्यात्वा विदितोऽपि प्रशाम्यति ॥ १२२ ॥
vastvantarE vEdyamAnE sarvavastuShu shUnyatA 
tAmEva manasA dhyAtvA viditOapi prashAmyati  122 

While perceiving an object, gradually become aware
of the emptiness within all things.
Meditating on this brings deep peace.

किंचिज्ज्ञैर्या स्मृता शुद्धिः सा शुद्धिः शम्भुदर्शने ।
न शुचिर्ह्यशुचिस्तस्मान्निर्विकल्पः सुखी भवेत् ॥ १२३ ॥
kiMchijjnairyA smRtA shuddhiH sA shuddhiH shambhudarshanE 
na shuchirhyashuchistasmAnnirvikalpaH sukhI bhavEt  123 

What ordinary people call purity
is considered impurity in the Śaiva vision.
There is neither purity nor impurity.
One who transcends such divisions becomes happy.

सर्वत्र भैरवो भावः सामान्येष्वपि गोचरः ।
न च तद्व्यतिरेकेण परोऽस्तीत्यद्वया गतिः ॥ १२४ ॥
sarvatra bhairavO bhAvaH sAmAnyEShvapi gOcharaH 
na cha tadvyatirEktENa parOastItyadvayA gatiH  124 

Bhairava exists everywhere and is accessible
even to ordinary people.
One who realizes that nothing exists apart from Him
attains non-duality.

समः शत्रौ च मित्रे च समो मानावमानयोः ॥
ब्रह्मणः परिपूर्णत्वातिति ज्ञात्वा सुखी भवेत् ॥ १२५ ॥
samaH shatrau cha mitrE cha samO mAnAvamAnayOH 
brahmaNaH paripUrNatvAtiti jnAtvA sukhI bhavEt  125 

One who remains equal toward friend and enemy,
honor and insult, knowing Brahman to be complete
and perfect, remains joyful.

न द्वेषं भावयेत्क्वापि न रागं भावयेत्क्वचित् ।
रागद्वेषविनिर्मुक्तौ मध्ये ब्रह्म प्रसर्पति ॥ १२६ ॥
na dvEShaM bhAvayEtkvApi na rAgaM bhAvayEtkvachit 
rAgadvEShavinirmuktau madhyE brahma prasarpati  126 

One should neither cultivate attachment nor hatred.
When both are absent, Brahman reveals itself
in the center between them.

यदवेद्यं यदग्राह्यं यच्छून्यं यदभावगम् ।
तत्सर्वं भैरवं भाव्यं तदन्ते बोधसम्भवः ॥ १२७ ॥
yadavEdyaM yadagrAhyaM yachChUnyaM yadabhAvagam 
tatsarvaM bhairavaM bhAvyaM tadantE bOdhasambhavaH  127 

That which cannot be known, grasped, or defined,
that emptiness beyond being, should be contemplated as Bhairava.
From this arises awakened consciousness.

नित्ये निराश्रये शून्ये व्यापके कलनोज्झिते ।
बाह्याकाशे मनः कृत्वा निराकाशं समाविशेत् ॥ १२८ ॥
nityE nirAshrayE shUnyE vyApakE kalanOjjhitE 
bAhyAkAshE manaH kRtvA nirAkAshaM samAvishEt  128 

Fixing the mind on the vast outer space: eternal, unsupported,
empty, and boundless, one enters the transcendence beyond space itself.

यत्र यत्र मनो याति तत्तत्तेनैव तत्क्षणम् ।
परित्यज्यानवस्थित्या निस्तरङ्गस्ततो भवेत् ॥ १२९ ॥
yatra yatra manO yAti tattattEnaiva tatkShaNam 
parityajyAnavasthityA nistarangastatO bhavEt  129 

Wherever the mind goes,
immediately abandoning attachment to it,
one becomes free from mental waves and disturbances.

भया सर्वं रवयति सर्वदो व्यापकोऽखिले ।
इति भैरवशब्दस्य सन्ततोच्चारणाच्छिवः ॥ १३० ॥
bhayA sarvaM ravayati sarvadO vyApakOakhilE 
iti bhairavashabdasya santatOchchAraNAchChivaH  130 

Because He makes everything vibrate through his radiance,
and because He pervades everything always,
through the constant repetition of the word “Bhairava,”
one becomes Shiva.

अहं ममेदमित्यादि प्रतिपत्तिप्रसङ्गतः ।
निराधारे मनो याति तद्ध्यानप्रेरणाच्छमी ॥ १३१ ॥
ahaM mamEdamityAdi pratipattiprasangataH 
nirAdhArE manO yAti taddhyAnaprEraNAchChamI  131 

While thinking “I,” “mine,” and similar notions,
the mind actually moves toward the unsupported ground
of pure awareness. Through meditation on this,
one becomes peaceful.

नित्यो विभुर्निराधारो व्यापकश्चाखिलाधिपः ।
शब्दान् प्रतिक्षणं ध्यायन् कृतार्थोऽर्थानुरूपतः ॥ १३२ ॥
nityO vibhurnirAdhArO vyApakashchAkhilAdhipaH 
shabdAn pratikShaNaM dhyAyan kRtArthOarthAnurUpataH  132 

Meditating constantly on words such as “eternal,”
“all-pervading,” “without support,” and “Lord of all,”
one realizes their truth directly.

अतत्त्वमिन्द्रजालाभमिदं सर्वमवस्थितम् ।
किं तत्त्वमिन्द्रजालस्य इति दार्ढ्याच्छमं व्रजेत् ॥ १३३ ॥
atattvamindrajAlAbhamidaM sarvamavasthitam 
kiM tattvamindrajAlasya iti dArDhyAchChamaM vrajEt  133 

All this exists like a magical illusion,
devoid of ultimate reality.
“What is the true essence of this illusion?”
By firmly contemplating thus, one attains peace.

आत्मनो निर्विकारस्य क्व ज्ञानं क्व च वा क्रिया ।
ज्ञानायत्ता बहिर्भावा अतः शून्यमिदं जगत् ॥ १३४ ॥
AtmanO nirvikArasya kva jnAnaM kva cha vA kriyA 
jnAnAyattA bahirbhAvA ataH shUnyamidaM jagat  134 

For the Self, which is changeless,
where is knowledge, and where is action?
External phenomena depend upon knowledge;
therefore this world is void in nature.

न मे बन्धो न मोक्षो मे भीतस्यैता विभीषिकाः ।
प्रतिबिम्बमिदं बुद्धेर्जलेष्विव विवस्वतः ॥ १३५ ॥
na mE bandhO na mOkShO mE bhItasyaitA vibhIShikAH 
pratibimbamidaM buddhErjalEShviva vivasvataH  135 

I have neither bondage nor liberation.
These are terrors only for the fearful.
This is but a reflection within the intellect,
like the reflection of the sun in water.

इन्द्रियद्वारकं सर्वं सुखदुःखादिसंगमम् ।
इतीन्द्रियाणि संत्यज्य स्वस्थः स्वात्मनि वर्तते ॥ १३६ ॥
indriyadvArakaM sarvaM sukhaduHkhAdisaMgamam 
itIndriyANi saMtyajya svasthaH svAtmani vartatE  136 

All contact with pleasure, pain, and the like
arises through the gateways of the senses.
Abandoning identification with the senses,
one rests established in one’s own Self.

ज्ञानप्रकाशकं सर्वं सर्वेणात्मा प्रकाशकः ।
एकमेकस्वभावत्वात्ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं विभाव्यते ॥ १३७ ॥
jnAnaprakAshakaM sarvaM sarvENAtmA prakAshakaH 
EkamEkasvabhAvatvAtjnAnaM jnEyaM vibhAvyatE  137 

Everything is illuminated by knowledge,
and the Self illuminates everything.
Because both are of one single nature,
knowledge and the known are understood as one.

मानसं चेतना शक्तिरात्मा चेति चतुष्टयम् ।
यदा प्रिये परिक्षीणं तदा तद्भैरवं वपुः ॥ १३८ ॥
mAnasaM chEtanA shaktirAtmA chEti chatuShTayam 
yadA priyE parikShINaM tadA tadbhairavaM vapuH  138 

Mind, consciousness, Shakti, and the Self,
O Beloved, when these four become dissolved,
that indeed is the form of Bhairava.

निस्तरङ्गोपदेशानां शतमुक्तं समासतः ।
द्वादशाभ्यधिकं देवि यज्ज्ञात्वा ज्ञानविज्जनः ॥ १३९ ॥
nistarangOpadEshAnAM shatamuktaM samAsataH 
dvAdashAbhyadhikaM dEvi yajjnAtvA jnAnavijjanaH  139 

Thus, in summary, one hundred and twelve teachings
for attaining the wave-less state have been spoken, O Devi.
Knowing these, one becomes a knower of Reality.

अत्र चैकतमे युक्तो जायते भैरवः स्वयम् ।
वाचा करोति कर्माणि शापानुग्रहकारकः ॥ १४० ॥
atra chaikatamE yuktO jAyatE bhairavaH svayam 
vAchA karOti karmANi shApAnugrahakArakaH  140 

Established firmly in even one of these methods,
one becomes Bhairava Himself.
Through speech, such a one performs acts
capable of both curse and blessing.

अजरामरतामेति सोऽणिमादिगुणान्वितः ।
योगिनीनां प्रियो देवि सर्वमेलापकाधिपः ॥ १४१ ॥
ajarAmaratAmEti sOaNimAdiguNAnvitaH 
yOginInAM priyO dEvi sarvamElApakAdhipaH  141 

One attains freedom from old age and death,
endowed with powers such as aṇimā and the rest.
O Devi, such a one becomes dear to the Yoginīs
and the lord of all gatherings.

जीवन्नपि विमुक्तोऽसौ कुर्वन्नपि न लिप्यते ।
jIvannapi vimuktOasau kurvannapi na lipyatE 

Even while living, that one is liberated,
and though acting, remains untouched.

श्री देव्युवाच ।
shrI dEvyuvAcha 

The auspicious Goddess spoke:

इदं यदि वपुर्देव परायाश्च महेश्वर ॥ १४२ ॥
idaM yadi vapurdEva parAyAshcha mahEshvara  142 

O Deva, O Maheshvara,
if this indeed is the form of the Supreme Goddess,

एवमुक्तव्यवस्थायां जप्यते को जपश्च कः ।
ध्यायते को महानाथ पूज्यते कश्च तृप्यति ॥ १४३ ॥
हूयते कस्य वा होमो यागः कस्य च किं कथम् ।
EvamuktavyavasthAyAM japyatE kO japashcha kaH 
dhyAyatE kO mahAnAtha pUjyatE kashcha tRpyati  143 
hUyatE kasya vA hOmO yAgaH kasya cha kiM katham 

then in such a state as you have described,
who performs japa, and what is japa even?
Who meditates, O Great Lord?
Who is worshipped, and who is satisfied?
For whom is the homa offered,
and to whom is sacrifice made, and how?

श्री भैरव उवाच ।
shrI bhairava uvAcha 

The auspicious Bhairava spoke:

एषात्र प्रक्रिया बाह्या स्थूलेष्वेव मृगेक्षणे ॥ १४४ ॥
EShAtra prakriyA bAhyA sthUlEShvEva mRgEkShaNE  144 

All these practices are external,
O doe-eyed One,
meant only for the gross level.

भूयो भूयः परे भावे भावना भाव्यते हि या ।
जपः सोऽत्र स्वयं नादो मन्त्रात्मा जप्य ईदृशः ॥ १४५ ॥
bhUyO bhUyaH parE bhAvE bhAvanA bhAvyatE hi yA 
japaH sOatra svayaM nAdO mantrAtmA japya IdRshaH  145 

True mantra repetition is the repeated absorption
into the supreme state.
The mantra is the inner sound of reality itself.

ध्यानं हि निश्चला बुद्धिर्निराकारा निराश्रया ।
न तु ध्यानं शरीराक्षिमुखहस्तादिकल्पना ॥ १४६ ॥
dhyAnaM hi nishchalA buddhirnirAkArA nirAshrayA 
na tu dhyAnaM sharIrAkShimukhahastAdikalpanA  146 

True meditation is the steady, formless, unsupported awareness.
It is not merely imagining a deity with
body, faces, hands, and ornaments.

पूजा नाम न पुष्पाद्यैर्या मतिः क्रियते दृढा ।
निर्विकल्पे महाव्योम्नि सा पूजा ह्यादराल्लयः ॥ १४७ ॥
pUjA nAma na puShpAdyairyA matiH kriyatE dRDhA 
nirvikalpE mahAvyOmni sA pUjA hyAdarAllayaH  147 

Real worship is not offering flowers and rituals.
It is firmly stabilizing the mind in the vast
thought-free sky of consciousness.
Through such worship, the mind dissolves.

अत्रैकतमयुक्तिस्थे योत्पद्येत दिनाद्दिनम् ।
भरिताकारता सात्र तृप्तिरत्यन्तपूर्णता ॥ १४८ ॥
atraikatamayuktisthE yOtpadyEta dinAddinam 
bharitAkAratA sAtra tRptiratyantapUrNatA  148 

For one established in even a single one of these methods,
there arises day by day a state of complete fullness.
That alone is satisfaction, the utter fullness of being.

महाशून्यालये वह्नौ भूताक्षविषयादिकम् ।
हूयते मनसा सार्धं स होमश्चेतनास्रुचा ॥ १४९ ॥
mahAshUnyAlayE vahnau bhUtAkShaviShayAdikam 
hUyatE manasA sArdhaM sa hOmashchEtanAsruchA  149 

Real sacred offering is pouring all elements, senses, objects,
and the mind itself into the fire of the Great Void
using awareness as the offering spoon.
The true homa is blissful fulfillment.

यागोऽत्र परमेशानि तुष्टिरानन्दलक्षणा ।
क्षपणात्सर्वपापानां त्राणात्सर्वस्य पार्वति ॥ १५० ॥
yAgOatra paramEshAni tuShTirAnandalakShaNA 
kShapaNAtsarvapApAnAM trANAtsarvasya pArvati  150 

O Supreme Goddess, the true Yaga here
is the satisfaction marked by bliss.
Because it destroys all sins and protects all beings, O Pārvatī.

रुद्रशक्तिसमावेशस्तत्क्षेत्रं भावना परा ।
अन्यथा तस्य तत्त्वस्य का पूजा काश्च तृप्यति ॥ १५१ ॥
rudrashaktisamAvEshastatkShEtraM bhAvanA parA 
anyathA tasya tattvasya kA pUjA kAshcha tRpyati  151 

Absorption into the union of Rudra and Shakti,
that indeed is the sacred field, the supreme contemplation.
Otherwise, what worship can there be
for that Reality, and who indeed can be satisfied?

स्वतन्त्रानन्दचिन्मात्रसारः स्वात्मा हि सर्वतः ।
आवेशनं तत्स्वरूपे स्वात्मनः स्नानमीरितम् ॥ १५२ ॥
svatantrAnandachinmAtrasAraH svAtmA hi sarvataH 
AvEshanaM tatsvarUpE svAtmanaH snAnamIritam  152 

The Self everywhere is of the essence of independent bliss
and pure consciousness alone.
Immersion of oneself into that very nature of the Self
is said to be the true sacred bath.

यैरेव पूज्यते द्रव्यैस्तर्प्यते वा परापरः ।
यश्चैव पूजकः सर्वः स एवैकः क्व पूजनम् ॥ १५३ ॥
yairEva pUjyatE dravyaistarpyatE vA parAparaH 
yashchaiva pUjakaH sarvaH sa EvaikaH kva pUjanam  153 

The very substances by which the Supreme
and the non-Supreme are worshipped or satisfied,
and the worshipper himself, are all That alone.
When only the One exists, where can there be worship?

व्रजेत्प्राणो विशेज्जीव इच्छया कुटिलाकृतिः ।
दीर्घात्मा सा महादेवी परक्षेत्रं परापरा ॥ १५४ ॥
vrajEtprANO vishEjjIva ichChayA kuTilAkRtiH 
dIrghAtmA sA mahAdEvI parakShEtraM parAparA  154 

The outgoing and incoming breath move according to divine will.
This life-force itself is the Great Goddess Kuṇḍalinī,
both transcendent and immanent,
and she is the supreme sacred field.

अस्यामनुचरन् तिष्ठन्महानन्दमयेऽध्वरे ।
तया देव्या समाविष्टः परं भैरवमाप्नुयात् ॥ १५५ ॥
asyAmanucharan tiShThanmahAnandamayEadhvarE 
tayA dEvyA samAviShTaH paraM bhairavamApnuyAt  155 

Following this path,
abiding in this practice filled with great bliss,
one who becomes absorbed in that Goddess
attains supreme Bhairava.

षट्शतानि दिवा रात्रौ सहस्राण्येकविंशतिः ।
जपो देव्याः समुद्दिष्टः सुलभो दुर्लभो जडैः ॥ १५६ ॥
ShaTshatAni divA rAtrau sahasrANyEkaviMshatiH 
japO dEvyAH samuddiShTaH sulabhO durlabhO jaDaiH  156 

This natural mantra repetition of the Goddess
occurs 21,600 times every day and night
through the breath itself.
It is easy for the aware, difficult for the dull-minded.

इत्येतत्कथितं देवि परमामृतमुत्तमम् ।
एतच्च नैव कस्यापि प्रकाश्यं तु कदाचन ॥ १५७ ॥
ityEtatkathitaM dEvi paramAmRtamuttamam 
Etachcha naiva kasyApi prakAshyaM tu kadAchana  157 

Thus, O Devi, this supreme nectar,
the highest teaching, has been spoken.
And this should never be initiated to just anyone.

परशिष्ये खले क्रूरे अभक्ते गुरुपादयोः ।
निर्विकल्पमतीनां तु वीराणामुन्नतात्मनाम् ॥ १५८ ॥
parashiShyE khalE krUrE abhaktE gurupAdayOH 
nirvikalpamatInAM tu vIrANAmunnatAtmanAm  158 

Not to another’s disciple,
nor to the wicked, the cruel,
or the one lacking devotion towards Guru’s feet.
But for the valiant, of elevated nature,
whose minds are free from conceptual thought,

भक्तानां गुरुवर्गस्य दातव्यं निर्विशङ्कया ।
ग्रामो राज्यं पुरं देशः पुत्रदारकुटुम्बकम् ॥ १५९ ॥
bhaktAnAM guruvargasya dAtavyaM nirvishankayA 
grAmO rAjyaM puraM dEshaH putradArakuTumbakam  159 

and for devoted ones,
within the lineage of the Guru,
this initiation may be given without hesitation.
In fact, a Village, Kingdom, City,
Country, Children, Spouse, and Family...

सर्वमेतत्परित्यज्य ग्राह्यमेतन्मृगेक्षणे ।
किमेभिरस्थिरैर्देवि स्थिरं परमिदं धनम् ।
प्राणा अपि प्रदातव्या न देयं परमामृतम् ॥ १६० ॥
sarvamEtatparityajya grAhyamEtanmRgEkShaNE 
kimEbhirasthirairdEvi sthiraM paramidaM dhanam 
prANA api pradAtavyA na dEyaM paramAmRtam  160 

all these should be set aside,
for the sake of attaining This, O doe-eyed One!
What use are these unstable things, O Devi,
when this alone is the stable and supreme treasure?
Even one’s life-breath may be given up,
but this supreme nectar must never be discarded.

श्री देव्युवाच ।
shrI dEvyuvAcha 

The auspicious Goddess spoke:

देवदेव माहदेव परितृप्तास्मि शङ्कर ।
रुद्रयामलतन्त्रस्य सारमद्यावधारितम् ॥ १६१ ॥
dEvadEva mAhadEva paritRptAsmi shankara 
rudrayAmalatantrasya sAramadyAvadhAritam  161 

O God of gods, O Great Lord,
I am completely satisfied, O Shankara.
Today I have understood
the essence of the Rudra Yamala Tantra.

सर्वशक्तिप्रभेदानां हृदयं ज्ञातमद्य च ।
इत्युक्त्वानन्दिता देवि कण्ठे लग्ना शिवस्य तु ॥ १६२ ॥
sarvashaktiprabhEdAnAM hRdayaM jnAtamadya cha 
ityuktvAnanditA dEvi kaNThE lagnA shivasya tu  162 

And today, I have known the very heart
of all the divisions of Shakti.
Having spoken thus, the delighted Goddess
embraced Shiva with Her arms around his neck!

इति श्री विज्ञानभैरव तन्त्रम् सम्पूर्णम्
iti shrI vijnAnabhairava tantram sampUrNam

Thus, the auspicious Vijnana Bhairava Tantra, is complete.
