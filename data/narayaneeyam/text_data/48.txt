ॐ नमो नारायणाय
AUM namo nArAyaNAya

अथ श्रीमन्नारायणीयम्
atha shrImannArAyaNIyam

And now the highly auspicious shlokas of Narayana

अष्टचत्वारिंशम् दशकम्
aShTachatvAriMsham dashakam

The Forty-eighth group of Ten shlokas

नलकूबर मणिग्रीवयो: शाप मोक्ष:
nalakUbara maNigrIvayoH shApa mokShaH

Lifting the Curse of Nalakubara and Manigreeva

मुदा सुरौघैस्त्वमुदारसम्मदैरुदीर्य दामोदर इत्यभिष्टुत: ।
मृदूदर: स्वैरमुलूखले लगन्नदूरतो द्वौ ककुभावुदैक्षथा: ॥१॥
mudA suraughaistvamudArasammadairudIrya dAmodara ityabhiShTutaH 
mRdUdaraH svairamulUkhale lagannadUrato dvau kakubhAvudaikShathAH 1

मुदा सुरौघैस्त्वमुदारसम्मदैरुदीर्य दामोदर इत्यभिष्टुत: ।
O One who was praised as Damodara, by the delighted groups of Devas in great joy,
मृदूदर: स्वैरमुलूखले लगन्नदूरतो द्वौ ककुभावुदैक्षथा: ॥१॥
with your tender belly tied up to the mortar, you saw two Arjuna trees at a distance!

कुबेरसूनुर्नलकूबराभिध: परो मणिग्रीव इति प्रथां गत: ।
महेशसेवाधिगतश्रियोन्मदौ चिरं किल त्वद्विमुखावखेलताम् ॥२॥
kuberasUnurnalakUbarAbhidhaH paro maNigrIva iti prathAM gataH 
maheshasevAdhigatashriyonmadau chiraM kila tvadvimukhAvakhelatAm 2

कुबेरसूनुर्नलकूबराभिध: परो मणिग्रीव इति प्रथां गत: ।
There were two renowned sons of Kubera by the names of Nalakubara and Manigriva.
महेशसेवाधिगतश्रियोन्मदौ चिरं किल त्वद्विमुखावखेलताम् ॥२॥
Becoming arrogant through enjoying the riches for a long time, obtained through the worship of Shiva, they developed a dislike for you and became unruly!

सुरापगायां किल तौ मदोत्कटौ सुरापगायद्बहुयौवतावृतौ ।
विवाससौ केलिपरौ स नारदो भवत्पदैकप्रवणो निरैक्षत ॥३॥
surApagAyAM kila tau madotkaTau surApagAyadbahuyauvatAvRtau 
vivAsasau keliparau sa nArado bhavatpadaikapravaNo niraikShata 3

सुरापगायां किल तौ मदोत्कटौ सुरापगायद्बहुयौवतावृतौ ।
In the divine river Ganga, didn't the both of them, as they were intoxicated with drink, surrounded by young women,
विवाससौ केलिपरौ स नारदो भवत्पदैकप्रवणो निरैक्षत ॥३॥
completely naked and making merry, didn't Sage Narada who is in constant devotion to your feet alone, see them!

भिया प्रियालोकमुपात्तवाससं पुरो निरीक्ष्यापि मदान्धचेतसौ ।
इमौ भवद्भक्त्युपशान्तिसिद्धये मुनिर्जगौ शान्तिमृते कुत: सुखम् ॥४॥
bhiyA priyAlokamupAttavAsasaM puro nirIkShyApi madAndhachetasau 
imau bhavadbhaktyupashAntisiddhaye munirjagau shAntimRte kutaH sukham 4

भिया प्रियालोकमुपात्तवाससं पुरो निरीक्ष्यापि मदान्धचेतसौ ।
As the women, afraid, ran to put their clothes back on, the two of them, despite seeing him in front of them, stayed there, blinded by intoxication.
इमौ भवद्भक्त्युपशान्तिसिद्धये मुनिर्जगौ शान्तिमृते कुत: सुखम् ॥४॥
In order to bestow upon them devotion and peace, the sage spoke, where is there happiness without peace!

युवामवाप्तौ ककुभात्मतां चिरं हरिं निरीक्ष्याथ पदं स्वमाप्नुताम् ।
इतीरितौ तौ भवदीक्षणस्पृहां गतौ व्रजान्ते ककुभौ बभूवतु: ॥५॥
yuvAmavAptau kakubhAtmatAM chiraM hariM nirIkShyAtha padaM svamApnutam 
itIretau tau bhavadIkShaNaspRhAM gatau vrajAnte kakubhau babhUvatuH 5

युवामवाप्तौ ककुभात्मतां चिरं हरिं निरीक्ष्याथ पदं स्वमाप्नुताम् ।
"The both of you, attaining to the forms of Arjuna trees, stay in waiting for Hari, and seeing him then attain to your own forms!"
इतीरितौ तौ भवदीक्षणस्पृहां गतौ व्रजान्ते ककुभौ बभूवतु: ॥५॥
thus as they were spoken to, they took the forms of both those trees at the outskirts of Vraja and awaited your sight!

अतन्द्रमिन्द्रद्रुयुगं तथाविधं समेयुषा मन्थरगामिना त्वया ।
तिरायितोलूखलरोधनिर्धुतौ चिराय जीर्णौ परिपातितौ तरू ॥६॥
atandramindradruyugaM tathAvidhaM sameyuShA mantharagAminA tvayA 
tirAyitolUkhalarodhanirdhutau chirAya jIrNau paripAtitau tarU 6

अतन्द्रमिन्द्रद्रुयुगं तथाविधं समेयुषा मन्थरगामिना त्वया ।
Without stopping, as you slowly passed through both those trees which stood thus side by side,
तिरायितोलूखलरोधनिर्धुतौ चिराय जीर्णौ परिपातितौ तरू ॥६॥
in the way of the mortar which was being dragged sideways, those long decayed trees then fell to the ground!

अभाजि शाखिद्वितयं यदा त्वया तदैव तद्गर्भतलान्निरेयुषा ।
महात्विषा यक्षयुगेन तत्क्षणादभाजि गोविन्द भवानपि स्तवै: ॥७॥
abhAji shAkhidvitayaM yadA tvayA tadaiva tadgarbhatalAnnireyuShA 
mahAtviShA yakShayugena tatkShaNAdabhAji govinda bhavAnapi stavaiH 7

अभाजि शाखिद्वितयं यदा त्वया तदैव तद्गर्भतलान्निरेयुषा ।
When both those trees were felled by you, at that moment, from their core, surfaced
महात्विषा यक्षयुगेन तत्क्षणादभाजि गोविन्द भवानपि स्तवै: ॥७॥
those pair of radiant Yakshas, and you too then shined, O Govinda, with their hymns of praise!

इहान्यभक्तोऽपि समेष्यति क्रमात् भवन्तमेतौ खलु रुद्रसेवकौ ।
मुनिप्रसादाद्भवदङ्घ्रिमागतौ गतौ वृणानौ खलु भक्तिमुत्तमाम् ॥८॥
ihAnyabhaktopi sameShyati kramAt bhavantametau khalu rudrasevakau 
muniprasAdAdbhavdanghrimAgatau gatau vRNAnau khalu bhaktimuttamAm 8

इहान्यभक्तोऽपि समेष्यति क्रमात् भवन्तमेतौ खलु रुद्रसेवकौ ।
In this world, even the devotees of others gradually reach you! These two spoilt devotees of rudra,
मुनिप्रसादाद्भवदङ्घ्रिमागतौ गतौ वृणानौ खलु भक्तिमुत्तमाम् ॥८॥
with the grace of the sage, didn't they reach your feet and then move on, praying for highest devotion!

ततस्तरूद्दारणदारुणारवप्रकम्पिसम्पातिनि गोपमण्डले ।
विलज्जितत्वज्जननीमुखेक्षिणा व्यमोक्षि नन्देन भवान् विमोक्षद: ॥९॥
tatastarUddAraNadAruNAravaprakampisampAtini gopamaNDale 
vilajjitatvajjananImukhekShiNA vyamokShi nandena bhavAn vimokShadaH 9

ततस्तरूद्दारणदारुणारवप्रकम्पिसम्पातिनि गोपमण्डले ।
As the terrible sound of the trees crashing frightened and caused tremors across the groups of Gopas,
विलज्जितत्वज्जननीमुखेक्षिणा व्यमोक्षि नन्देन भवान् विमोक्षद: ॥९॥
your mother, ashamed at her face being looked at by Nanda, released you, O One Who Grants Liberation!

महीरुहोर्मध्यगतो बतार्भको हरे: प्रभावादपरिक्षतोऽधुना ।
इति ब्रुवाणैर्गमितो गृहं भवान् मरुत्पुराधीश्वर पाहि मां गदात् ॥१०॥
mahIruhormadhyagato batArbhako hareH prabhAvAdaparikShatodhunA 
iti bruvANairgamito gRhaM bhavAn marutpurAdhIshvara pAhi mAM gadAt 10

महीरुहोर्मध्यगतो बतार्भको हरे: प्रभावादपरिक्षतोऽधुना ।
"Say this little child who passed in between those falling trees, has certainly been saved by the grace of Hari!" 
इति ब्रुवाणैर्गमितो गृहं भवान् मरुत्पुराधीश्वर पाहि मां गदात् ॥१०॥
with these utterances you were taken back into the house again, O Lord of the City of Wind! Protect me from my disease!
