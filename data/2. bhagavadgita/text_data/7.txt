॥ ॐ श्री परमात्मने नमः ॥
AUM shrI paramAtmane namaH

I bow down to the Supreme Consciousness

॥ अथ श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता ॥
atha shrImadbhagavadgItA

And Now, We start the Holy Bhagavadgita

अथ सप्तमोऽध्यायः ।   ज्ञानविज्ञानयोगः
atha saptamoadhyAyaH    jnAnavijnAnayogaH

Thus starts the seventh chapter of Bhagavad Gita, which describes jnAnavijnAna yogaH, where Lord Krishna gives Arjuna the knowledge and the methodology through which one can attain to the true knowledge of the "self".

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
shrIbhagavAnuvAcha

Lord Krishna spoke thus:

मय्यासक्तमनाः पार्थ योगं युञ्जन्मदाश्रयः ।
असंशयं समग्रं मां यथा ज्ञास्यसि तच्छृणु ॥ ७-१॥
mayyAsaktamanAH pArtha yogaM yunjanmadAshrayaH 
asanshayaM samagraM mAn yathA jnAsyasi tachChRNu  7.1

O arjuna, Now hear the ways in which those whose minds are invested in "Me", who take refuge in "Me", and strive towards yoga, attain to "Me" surely and completely.

ज्ञानं तेऽहं सविज्ञानमिदं वक्ष्याम्यशेषतः ।
यज्ज्ञात्वा नेह भूयोऽन्यज्ज्ञातव्यमवशिष्यते ॥ ७-२॥
jnAnaM teahaM savijnAnamidaM vakShyAmyasheShataH 
yajjnAtvA neha bhUyoanyajjnAtavyamavashiShyate  7.2

I shall now speak to you fully about this knowledge and methodology, knowing which nothing else remains to be known in this world.

मनुष्याणां सहस्रेषु कश्चिद्यतति सिद्धये ।
यततामपि सिद्धानां कश्चिन्मां वेत्ति तत्त्वतः ॥ ७-३॥
manuShyANAn sahasreShu kashchidyatati siddhaye 
yatatAmapi siddhAnAn kashchinmAn vetti tattvataH  7.3

Among thousands of human beings, hardly one person strives for this kind of attainment; 
and among those who strive towards it, hardly one person gets to realize "Me" as the eternal unity.

भूमिरापोऽनलो वायुः खं मनो बुद्धिरेव च ।
अहङ्कार इतीयं मे भिन्ना प्रकृतिरष्टधा ॥ ७-४॥
bhUmirApoanalo vAyuH khaM mano buddhireva cha 
ahankAra itIyaM me bhinnA prakRtiraShTadhA  7.4

Earth, water, fire, air, space, mind, intellect, and the sense of self—
these are eightfold manifestations of my materialistic nature.

अपरेयमितस्त्वन्यां प्रकृतिं विद्धि मे पराम् ।
जीवभूतां महाबाहो ययेदं धार्यते जगत् ॥ ७-५॥
apareyamitastvanyAM prakRtin viddhi me parAm 
jIvabhUtAM mahAbAho yayedaM dhAryate jagat  7.5

O mighty armed Arjuna, there is another manifestation of "Me", the nature of which is beyond the ones I mentioned before.
This is the manifestation that resides in all of creation, and sustains it.

एतद्योनीनि भूतानि सर्वाणीत्युपधारय ।
अहं कृत्स्नस्य जगतः प्रभवः प्रलयस्तथा ॥ ७-६॥
etadyonIni bhUtAni sarvANItyupadhAraya 
ahaM kRtsnasya jagataH prabhavaH pralayastathA  7.6

Know that all living beings are born out of the coming together of these two natures of mine. 
I am the source of existence, and the destination of dissolution, for all of creation.

मत्तः परतरं नान्यत्किञ्चिदस्ति धनञ्जय ।
मयि सर्वमिदं प्रोतं सूत्रे मणिगणा इव ॥ ७-७॥
mattaH parataraM nAnyatkinchidasti dhananjaya 
mayi sarvamidaM protaM sUtre maNigaNA iva  7.7

There is nothing that is beyond my "Self", O Arjuna. 
Everything is held through "Me", as a group of beads are strung on a thread.

रसोऽहमप्सु कौन्तेय प्रभास्मि शशिसूर्ययोः ।
प्रणवः सर्ववेदेषु शब्दः खे पौरुषं नृषु ॥ ७-८॥
rasoahamapsu kaunteya prabhAsmi shashisUryayoH 
praNavaH sarvavedeShu shabdaH khe pauruShaM nRShu  7.8

I am the life essence in water, O son of Kunti, 
and the radiance of the sun and the moon. 
I am the cosmic sound, praNavaH in all of the vedas
and I am the sound in space, 
and I am the existence among humans.

पुण्यो गन्धः पृथिव्यां च तेजश्चास्मि विभावसौ ।
जीवनं सर्वभूतेषु तपश्चास्मि तपस्विषु ॥ ७-९॥
puNyo gandhaH pRthivyAn cha tejashchAsmi vibhAvasau 
jIvanaM sarvabhUteShu tapashchAsmi tapasviShu  7.9

I am the nourishing fragrance of the Earth, 
and the brilliance of the fire. 
I am the life-force in all beings, 
and the generated-glow of the ascetics.

बीजं मां सर्वभूतानां विद्धि पार्थ सनातनम् ।
बुद्धिर्बुद्धिमतामस्मि तेजस्तेजस्विनामहम् ॥ ७-१०॥
bIjaM mAn sarvabhUtAnAn viddhi pArtha sanAtanam 
buddhirbuddhimatAmasmi tejastejasvinAmaham  7.10

O Arjuna, know that I am the eternal seed of all beings. 
I am the intellect of the intelligent, 
and the splendor of the glorious.

बलं बलवतां चाहं कामरागविवर्जितम् ।
धर्माविरुद्धो भूतेषु कामोऽस्मि भरतर्षभ ॥ ७-११॥
balaM balavatAn chAhaM kAmarAgavivarjitam 
dharmAviruddho bhUteShu kAmoasmi bharatarShabha  7.11

O best of the Bharatas, I am the strength of the strong, which is devoid of entanglements of lust. 
I am the sense of desire that is not against the sustainance of life.

ये चैव सात्त्विका भावा राजसास्तामसाश्च ये ।
मत्त एवेति तान्विद्धि न त्वहं तेषु ते मयि ॥ ७-१२॥
ye chaiva sAttvikA bhAvA rAjasAstAmasAshcha ye 
matta eveti tAnviddhi na tvahaM teShu te mayi  7.12

All of those states of existence that belong to sattva, rajas and tamas; know that they are born out of "Me".
I and not within them.

त्रिभिर्गुणमयैर्भावैरेभिः सर्वमिदं जगत् ।
मोहितं नाभिजानाति मामेभ्यः परमव्ययम् ॥ ७-१३॥
tribhirguNamayairbhAvairebhiH sarvamidan jagat 
mohitaM nAbhijAnAti mAmebhyaH paramavyayam  7.13

Deluded by the three modes of Maya, the people in this world are unable to know me as the self that is imperishable and eternal.

दैवी ह्येषा गुणमयी मम माया दुरत्यया ।
मामेव ये प्रपद्यन्ते मायामेतां तरन्ति ते ॥ ७-१४॥
daivI hyeShA guNamayI mama mAyA duratyayA 
mAmeva ye prapadyante mAyAmetAM taranti te  7.14

This divine illusion of my "self", that is bound in the three modes of nature, is hard to overcome.
Those who take refuge in the true essence of "Me" alone are able to overcome this delusion. 

न मां दुष्कृतिनो मूढाः प्रपद्यन्ते नराधमाः ।
माययापहृतज्ञाना आसुरं भावमाश्रिताः ॥ ७-१५॥
na mAn duShkRtino mUDhAH prapadyante narAdhamAH 
mAyayApahRtajnAnA AsuraM bhAvamAshritAH  7.15

People of lower tendencies, who are ignorant of the true nature of "myself", do not strive towards knowing "Me".
They resort to baser tendencies since their knowledge is overcome by delusion.

चतुर्विधा भजन्ते मां जनाः सुकृतिनोऽर्जुन ।
आर्तो जिज्ञासुरर्थार्थी ज्ञानी च भरतर्षभ ॥ ७-१६॥
chaturvidhA bhajante mAn janAH sukRtinoarjuna 
Arto jijnAsurarthArthI jnAnI cha bharatarShabha  7.16

People of higher tendencies, who strive towards realizing "Me" fall into four categories O Arjuna.
Those who are distressed, those who are curious, those who seek to achieve something, and those who seek knowledge.

तेषां ज्ञानी नित्ययुक्त एकभक्तिर्विशिष्यते ।
प्रियो हि ज्ञानिनोऽत्यर्थमहं स च मम प्रियः ॥ ७-१७॥
teShAm jnAnI nityayukta ekabhaktirvishiShyate 
priyo hi jnAninoatyarthamahaM sa cha mama priyaH  7.17

Among these, those who seek "Me" for the sake of true knowledge, with constant devotion, are truly special.
They value "Me" very dearly and I value them very dearly. 

उदाराः सर्व एवैते ज्ञानी त्वात्मैव मे मतम् ।
आस्थितः स हि युक्तात्मा मामेवानुत्तमां गतिम् ॥ ७-१८॥
udArAH sarva evaite jnAnI tvAtmaiva me matam 
AsthitaH sa hi yuktAtmA mAmevAnuttamAn gatim  7.18

Indeed, all of them are sincere in their efforts. 
But those who seek knowledge, I consider to be the very embodiment of the "self".
Such people are truly established in "myself", and attain to the highest of states.

बहूनां जन्मनामन्ते ज्ञानवान्मां प्रपद्यते ।
वासुदेवः सर्वमिति स महात्मा सुदुर्लभः ॥ ७-१९॥
bahUnAm janmanAmante jnAnavAnmAM prapadyate 
vAsudevaH sarvamiti sa mahAtmA sudurlabhaH  7.19

After continued spiritual practices in many cycles of birth and death, one who seeks knowledge attains to "Me".
Such a person who realizes that it is I, "vAsudeva", who is everything that exists, is really rare to find.

कामैस्तैस्तैर्हृतज्ञानाः प्रपद्यन्तेऽन्यदेवताः ।
तं तं नियममास्थाय प्रकृत्या नियताः स्वया ॥ ७-२०॥
kAmaistaistairhRtajnAnAH prapadyanteanyadevatAH 
taM taM niyamamAsthAya prakRtyA niyatAH svayA  7.20

Others, whose knowledge is taken to various kinds of desires, take to the worship of various kinds of deities respectively.
Based on their individual natures, they perform practices that achieve their desires.

यो यो यां यां तनुं भक्तः श्रद्धयार्चितुमिच्छति ।
तस्य तस्याचलां श्रद्धां तामेव विदधाम्यहम् ॥ ७-२१॥
yo yo yAn yAn tanuM bhaktaH shraddhayArchitumichChati 
tasya tasyAchalAn shraddhAn tAmeva vidadhAmyaham  7.21

Whoever wishes to approach and worship whichever form or deity, I respond with the same focus and steadfastness back unto themselves. 

स तया श्रद्धया युक्तस्तस्याराधनमीहते ।
लभते च ततः कामान्मयैव विहितान्हि तान् ॥ ७-२२॥
sa tayA shraddhayA yuktastasyArAdhanamIhate 
labhate cha tataH kAmAnmayaiva vihitAnhi tAn  7.22

Such worship of those deities, performed by those people, with focus and steadfastness yields results accordingly.
Know, that it is from I alone that these results have originated.

अन्तवत्तु फलं तेषां तद्भवत्यल्पमेधसाम् ।
देवान्देवयजो यान्ति मद्भक्ता यान्ति मामपि ॥ ७-२३॥
antavattu phalaM teShAn tadbhavatyalpamedhasAm 
devAndevayajo yAnti madbhaktA yAnti mAmapi  7.23

Such results obtained by worshipping deities in this world, come with an end date.
Those who are of a lower understanding resort to the worship of such deities, but those who are interested in knowing the "self", resort to "Me".

अव्यक्तं व्यक्तिमापन्नं मन्यन्ते मामबुद्धयः ।
परं भावमजानन्तो ममाव्ययमनुत्तमम् ॥ ७-२४॥
avyaktaM vyaktimApannaM manyante mAmabuddhayaH 
paraM bhAvamajAnanto mamAvyayamanuttamam  7.24

Those without the right understanding think, that I am of this form. 
The truth is that I am indescribable.
They do not know of the true nature of "myself" which is ultimate and is of the beyond.

नाहं प्रकाशः सर्वस्य योगमायासमावृतः ।
मूढोऽयं नाभिजानाति लोको मामजमव्ययम् ॥ ७-२५॥
nAhaM prakAshaH sarvasya yogamAyAsamAvRtaH 
mUDhoayaM nAbhijAnAti loko mAmajamavyayam  7.25

I am not clearly evident to everyone because of the veil of illusion around the self. 
Those who are deluded cannot realize the nature of my true self which is unchanging and eternal.

वेदाहं समतीतानि वर्तमानानि चार्जुन ।
भविष्याणि च भूतानि मां तु वेद न कश्चन ॥ ७-२६॥
vedAhaM samatItAni vartamAnAni chArjuna 
bhaviShyANi cha bhUtAni mAn tu veda na kashchana  7.26

I know of that which has existed, that which exists, and that which is going to exist, O Arjuna.
But no one realizes the true nature of "Me".

इच्छाद्वेषसमुत्थेन द्वन्द्वमोहेन भारत ।
सर्वभूतानि सम्मोहं सर्गे यान्ति परन्तप ॥ ७-२७॥
ichChAdveShasamutthena dvandvamohena bhArata 
sarvabhUtAni sammohaM sarge yAnti parantapa  7.27

O destroyer of enemies, all creatures that are born, are deluded by the feelings of liking and aversion, which are caused due to the illusion of the dualities of existence.

येषां त्वन्तगतं पापं जनानां पुण्यकर्मणाम् ।
ते द्वन्द्वमोहनिर्मुक्ता भजन्ते मां दृढव्रताः ॥ ७-२८॥
yeShAn tvantagataM pApan janAnAM puNyakarmaNAm 
te dvandvamohanirmuktA bhajante mAn dRDhavratAH  7.28

But those people, whose faults have been destroyed by engaging in right practices, become free from the illusion of such dualities. 
Such people are established firmly in "myself".

जरामरणमोक्षाय मामाश्रित्य यतन्ति ये ।
ते ब्रह्म तद्विदुः कृत्स्नमध्यात्मं कर्म चाखिलम् ॥ ७-२९॥
jarAmaraNamokShAya mAmAshritya yatanti ye 
te brahma tadviduH kRtsnamadhyAtmaM karma chAkhilam  7.29

Those who strive for liberation from the cycles of birth and death, by taking refuge in "Me", they realize that divine, all pervading nature and activity of pure consciousness.

साधिभूताधिदैवं मां साधियज्ञं च ये विदुः ।
प्रयाणकालेऽपि च मां ते विदुर्युक्तचेतसः ॥ ७-३०॥
sAdhibhUtAdhidaivaM mAn sAdhiyajnan cha ye viduH 
prayANakAleapi cha mAn te viduryuktachetasaH  7.30

Those who know "Me" as the principle aspect of materialistic creation,
and the principle aspect of all divinity,
such people who are of an evolved consciousness realize the true nature of the "self", as they leave the body.

ॐ तत्सदिति श्रीमद्भगवद्गीतासूपनिषत्सु
ब्रह्मविद्यायां योगशास्त्रे श्रीकृष्णार्जुनसंवादे
ज्ञानविज्ञानयोगो नाम सप्तमोऽध्यायः ॥ ७॥
AUM tatsaditi shrImadbhagavadgItAsUpaniShatsu
brahmavidyAyAn yogashAstre shrIkRShNArjunasanvAde
jnAnavijnAnayogo nAma saptamoadhyAyaH  7

And thus ends the seventh chapter, by the name of jnAnavijnAna yogah.
This is taken from Shrimad Bhagavad Gita, a conversation between Lord Krishna and Arjuna on the science of yoga, which is the knowledge of ultimate reality, called as brahma vidya.
