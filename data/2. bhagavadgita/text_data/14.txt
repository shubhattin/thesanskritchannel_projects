॥ ॐ श्री परमात्मने नमः ॥
AUm shrI paramAtmane namaH

I bow down to the Supreme Consciousness

॥ अथ श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता ॥
atha shrImadbhagavadgItA

And Now, We start the Holy Bhagavadgita

अथ चतुर्दशोऽध्यायः ।   गुणत्रयविभागयोगः
atha chaturdashoadhyAyaH    guNatrayavibhAgayogaH

Thus starts the fourteenth chapter of Bhagavad Gita, called guNatrayavibhAga yogaH.

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
shrIbhagavAnuvAcha 

Continuing his explanation of the distinction between the physical and the non-physical, lord Krishna spoke the following words:

परं भूयः प्रवक्ष्यामि ज्ञानानां ज्ञानमुत्तमम् ।
यज्ज्ञात्वा मुनयः सर्वे परां सिद्धिमितो गताः ॥ १४-१॥
paraM bhUyaH pravakShyAmi jnAnAnAn jnAnamuttamam 
yajjnAtvA munayaH sarve parAM siddhimito gatAH  14.1

I shall once again speak to you of this best of knowledge; It is by knowing this that all the great sages attained to the highest of states.

इदं ज्ञानमुपाश्रित्य मम साधर्म्यमागताः ।
सर्गेऽपि नोपजायन्ते प्रलये न व्यथन्ति च ॥ १४-२॥
idaM jnAnamupAshritya mama sAdharmyamAgatAH 
sargeapi nopajAyante pralaye na vyathanti cha  14.2

Those who take refuge in this knowledge and realize the true sense of self, they shall not be reborn at the time of creation and nor be destroyed at the time of dissolution.

मम योनिर्महद् ब्रह्म तस्मिन्गर्भं दधाम्यहम् ।
सम्भवः सर्वभूतानां ततो भवति भारत ॥ १४-३॥
mama yonirmahad brahma tasmingarbhan dadhAmyaham 
sambhavaH sarvabhUtAnAn tato bhavati bhArata  14.3

The entire existence is the womb, and it is "I" who creates lifeforms in it.
And therefore, it is "I",who is the sense of existence of all creatures, O Arjuna. 

सर्वयोनिषु कौन्तेय मूर्तयः सम्भवन्ति याः ।
तासां ब्रह्म महद्योनिरहं बीजप्रदः पिता ॥ १४-४॥
sarvayoniShu kaunteya mUrtayaH sambhavanti yAH 
tAsAM brahma mahadyonirahaM bIjapradaH pitA  14.4

O Son of Kunti, the forms that take shape in all wombs, know that the vastness of creation is the original womb where they were all born, and "I" am the seed that gave them existence, and thus am their father.

सत्त्वं रजस्तम इति गुणाः प्रकृतिसम्भवाः ।
निबध्नन्ति महाबाहो देहे देहिनमव्ययम् ॥ १४-५॥
sattvaM rajastama iti guNAH prakRtisambhavAH 
nibadhnanti mahAbAho dehe dehinamavyayam  14.5

O mighty-armed Arjuna, the materialistic manifestation in-turn gives rise to three modes of existence, called sattva, rajas and tamas. These modes bind the imperishable self within the perishable body.

तत्र सत्त्वं निर्मलत्वात्प्रकाशकमनामयम् ।
सुखसङ्गेन बध्नाति ज्ञानसङ्गेन चानघ ॥ १४-६॥
tatra sattvaM nirmalatvAtprakAshakamanAmayam 
sukhasangena badhnAti jnAnasangena chAnagha  14.6

Among these, sattva guNa, being free from impurity, is illuminating and full of well-being. 
O Arjuna, it binds the self by creating attachment for a sense of happiness and knowledge.

रजो रागात्मकं विद्धि तृष्णासङ्गसमुद्भवम् ।
तन्निबध्नाति कौन्तेय कर्मसङ्गेन देहिनम् ॥ १४-७॥
rajo rAgAtmakaM viddhi tRShNAsangasamudbhavam 
tannibadhnAti kaunteya karmasangena dehinam  14.7

O Arjuna, rajo guNa is of the nature of passion. 
It arises from worldly desires and affections, and binds the self through attachment to activity that leads to results.

तमस्त्वज्ञानजं विद्धि मोहनं सर्वदेहिनाम् ।
प्रमादालस्यनिद्राभिस्तन्निबध्नाति भारत ॥ १४-८॥
tamastvajnAnajaM viddhi mohanaM sarvadehinAm 
pramAdAlasyanidrAbhistannibadhnAti bhArata  14.8

O Arjuna, tamo guNa, which is born of ignorance, is the cause of illusion for the embodied selfs. 
It deludes all living beings through negligence, laziness, and sleep.

सत्त्वं सुखे सञ्जयति रजः कर्मणि भारत ।
ज्ञानमावृत्य तु तमः प्रमादे सञ्जयत्युत ॥ १४-९॥
sattvaM sukhe sanjayati rajaH karmaNi bhArata 
jnAnamAvRtya tu tamaH pramAde sanjayatyuta  14.9

Sattva binds one to material happiness; rajas conditions the self toward actions; and tamas clouds wisdom and binds one in delusion.

रजस्तमश्चाभिभूय सत्त्वं भवति भारत ।
रजः सत्त्वं तमश्चैव तमः सत्त्वं रजस्तथा ॥ १४-१०॥
rajastamashchAbhibhUya sattvaM bhavati bhArata 
rajaH sattvaM tamashchaiva tamaH sattvaM rajastathA  14.10

Sometimes sattva prevails over rajas and tamas, O descendant of Bharata. 
Sometimes rajas dominates sattva tamas, and at other times tamas overcomes sattva and rajas.

सर्वद्वारेषु देहेऽस्मिन्प्रकाश उपजायते ।
ज्ञानं यदा तदा विद्याद्विवृद्धं सत्त्वमित्युत ॥ १४-११॥
sarvadvAreShu deheasminprakAsha upajAyate 
jnAnaM yadA tadA vidyAdvivRddhan sattvamityuta  14.11

When all the portals of the body are illuminated by true knowledge, know it to be a manifestation of the mode of sattva. 

लोभः प्रवृत्तिरारम्भः कर्मणामशमः स्पृहा ।
रजस्येतानि जायन्ते विवृद्धे भरतर्षभ ॥ १४-१२॥
lobhaH pravRttirArambhaH karmaNAmashamaH spRhA 
rajasyetAni jAyante vivRddhe bharatarShabha  14.12

When the mode of rajas predominates, O Arjuna, the symptoms of greed, exertion for worldly gain, restlessness, and craving develop.

अप्रकाशोऽप्रवृत्तिश्च प्रमादो मोह एव च ।
तमस्येतानि जायन्ते विवृद्धे कुरुनन्दन ॥ १४-१३॥
aprakAshoapravRttishcha pramAdo moha eva cha 
tamasyetAni jAyante vivRddhe kurunandana  14.13

O Arjuna, gloominess, inertia, negligence, and delusion—these are the dominant signs of the mode of tamas.

यदा सत्त्वे प्रवृद्धे तु प्रलयं याति देहभृत् ।
तदोत्तमविदां लोकानमलान्प्रतिपद्यते ॥ १४-१४॥
yadA sattve pravRddhe tu pralayaM yAti dehabhRt 
tadottamavidAn lokAnamalAnpratipadyate  14.14

When an embodied being passes away in the mode of sattva, one attains to higher states of being which are free of unpleasantness.

रजसि प्रलयं गत्वा कर्मसङ्गिषु जायते ।
तथा प्रलीनस्तमसि मूढयोनिषु जायते ॥ १४-१५॥
rajasi pralayaM gatvA karmasangiShu jAyate 
tathA pralInastamasi mUDhayoniShu jAyate  14.15

When one leaves the body in the mode of rajas, one is  reborn among people driven by work, while those who pass away in the mode of tamas take birth in a lower species in the series of evolution.

कर्मणः सुकृतस्याहुः सात्त्विकं निर्मलं फलम् ।
रजसस्तु फलं दुःखमज्ञानं तमसः फलम् ॥ १४-१६॥
karmaNaH sukRtasyAhuH sAttvikaM nirmalaM phalam 
rajasastu phalaM duHkhamajnAnaM tamasaH phalam  14.16

It is said that the fruits of actions performed in the mode of sattva are free of entanglement. 
Actions done in the mode of rajas result in suffering, while those performed in the mode of tamas result in ignorance.

सत्त्वात्सञ्जायते ज्ञानं रजसो लोभ एव च ।
प्रमादमोहौ तमसो भवतोऽज्ञानमेव च ॥ १४-१७॥
sattvAtsanjAyate jnAnaM rajaso lobha eva cha 
pramAdamohau tamaso bhavatoajnAnameva cha  14.17

From the mode of sattva arises knowledge, from the mode of rajas arises greed, and from the mode of tamas arise negligence and delusion.

ऊर्ध्वं गच्छन्ति सत्त्वस्था मध्ये तिष्ठन्ति राजसाः ।
जघन्यगुणवृत्तिस्था अधो गच्छन्ति तामसाः ॥ १४-१८॥
Urdhvan gachChanti sattvasthA madhye tiShThanti rAjasAH 
jaghanyaguNavRttisthA adho gachChanti tAmasAH  14.18

Those situated in the mode of sattva rise upward; those in the mode of rajas stay in the middle; and those in the mode of tamas move downwards in the process of evolution.

नान्यं गुणेभ्यः कर्तारं यदा द्रष्टानुपश्यति ।
गुणेभ्यश्च परं वेत्ति मद्भावं सोऽधिगच्छति ॥ १४-१९॥
nAnyan guNebhyaH kartAraM yadA draShTAnupashyati 
guNebhyashcha paraM vetti madbhAvaM soadhigachChati  14.19

When a wise person sees that all activity is infact inseparable from the modes of existence, and knows that there is a sense of being beyond the modes of materialistic nature, such a person attains to my true sense of self.

गुणानेतानतीत्य त्रीन्देही देहसमुद्भवान् ।
जन्ममृत्युजरादुःखैर्विमुक्तोऽमृतमश्नुते ॥ १४-२०॥
guNAnetAnatItya trIndehI dehasamudbhavAn 
janmamRtyujarAduHkhairvimuktoamRtamashnute  14.20

By transcending the three modes of materialistic nature associated with the body, one becomes free from birth, death, disease, old age, and misery, and attains to immortality.

अर्जुन उवाच ।
arjuna uvAcha 

And then, Arjuna asked the following question:

कैर्लिङ्गैस्त्रीन्गुणानेतानतीतो भवति प्रभो ।
किमाचारः कथं चैतांस्त्रीन्गुणानतिवर्तते ॥ १४-२१॥
kairlingaistrInguNAnetAnatIto bhavati prabho 
kimAchAraH kathan chaitAnstrInguNAnativartate  14.21

O Lord, What are the characteristics of a person who has gone beyond these three guṇas? 
How does such a person act, and how does one attain to such a state?

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
shrIbhagavAnuvAcha 

Lord Krishna replied:

प्रकाशं च प्रवृत्तिं च मोहमेव च पाण्डव ।
न द्वेष्टि सम्प्रवृत्तानि न निवृत्तानि काङ्क्षति ॥ १४-२२॥
prakAshan cha pravRttin cha mohameva cha pANDava 
na dveShTi sampravRttAni na nivRttAni kAnkShati  14.22

O pAnDava, such a person neither has a liking, nor aversion, for enlightenment, activity or even delusion.

उदासीनवदासीनो गुणैर्यो न विचाल्यते ।
गुणा वर्तन्त इत्येवं योऽवतिष्ठति नेङ्गते ॥ १४-२३॥
udAsInavadAsIno guNairyo na vichAlyate 
guNA vartanta ityevaM yoavatiShThati nengate  14.23

A person who is not disturbed either by the presence, or lack of the influence of guNas, but just acknowledges their existence, is truly established in the self without wavering.

समदुःखसुखः स्वस्थः समलोष्टाश्मकाञ्चनः ।
तुल्यप्रियाप्रियो धीरस्तुल्यनिन्दात्मसंस्तुतिः ॥ १४-२४॥
samaduHkhasukhaH svasthaH samaloShTAshmakAnchanaH 
tulyapriyApriyo dhIrastulyanindAtmasanstutiH  14.24

A person who is well-established within the self, is equanimous in happiness and sorrow,looks at a piece of stone, metal or gold with equanimity, remains in complete balance in pleasantness or unpleasantness, praise or blame.

मानापमानयोस्तुल्यस्तुल्यो मित्रारिपक्षयोः ।
सर्वारम्भपरित्यागी गुणातीतः स उच्यते ॥ १४-२५॥
mAnApamAnayostulyastulyo mitrAripakShayoH 
sarvArambhaparityAgI guNAtItaH sa uchyate  14.25

Such a person remains in complete balance in honour or dishonour, amidst friends or enemies.
A person who is devoid of compulsive desire to act is said to be beyond the materialistic modes of existence.

मां च योऽव्यभिचारेण भक्तियोगेन सेवते ।
स गुणान्समतीत्यैतान्ब्रह्मभूयाय कल्पते ॥ १४-२६॥
mAn cha yoavyabhichAreNa bhaktiyogena sevate 
sa guNAnsamatItyaitAnbrahmabhUyAya kalpate  14.26

A person who serves "Me" with an unwavering sense of devotion, called bhakti yoga, such a person, shall rise above the realm of the guNas and is united with the very source of creation.

ब्रह्मणो हि प्रतिष्ठाहममृतस्याव्ययस्य च ।
शाश्वतस्य च धर्मस्य सुखस्यैकान्तिकस्य च ॥ १४-२७॥
brahmaNo hi pratiShThAhamamRtasyAvyayasya cha 
shAshvatasya cha dharmasya sukhasyaikAntikasya cha  14.27

"I" am the basis of the source of creation, brahman, which has no form, is immortal and imperishable.
"I" am the basis of eternal dharma, and of unending divine bliss.

ॐ तत्सदिति श्रीमद्भगवद्गीतासूपनिषत्सु
ब्रह्मविद्यायां योगशास्त्रे श्रीकृष्णार्जुनसंवादे
गुणत्रयविभागयोगो नाम चतुर्दशोऽध्यायः ॥ १४॥
AUM tatsaditi shrImadbhagavadgItAsUpaniShatsu
brahmavidyAyAn yogashAstre shrIkRShNArjunasanvAde
guNatrayavibhAgayogo nAma chaturdashoadhyAyaH  14

And thus ends the fourteenth chapter, by the name of guNatrayavibhAga yogaH.
This is taken from Shrimad Bhagavad Gita, a conversation between Lord Krishna and Arjuna on the science of yoga, which is the knowledge of ultimate reality, called as brahma vidya.
