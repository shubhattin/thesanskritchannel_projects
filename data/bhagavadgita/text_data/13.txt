॥ ॐ श्री परमात्मने नमः ॥
AUm shrI paramAtmane namaH

I bow down to the Supreme Consciousness

॥ अथ श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता ॥
atha shrImadbhagavadgItA

And Now, We start the Holy Bhagavadgita

अथ त्रयोदशोऽध्यायः ।   क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञविभागयोगः
atha trayodashoadhyAyaH    kShetrakShetrajnavibhAgayogaH

Thus starts the thirteenth chapter of Bhagavad Gita, called kShetrakShetrajnavibhAga yogaH.

अर्जुन उवाच ।
arjuna uvAcha 

After having heard of the means of attaining to the divine through devotion, or bhakti, arjuna had further doubts, which he expressed thus:

प्रकृतिं पुरुषं चैव क्षेत्रं क्षेत्रज्ञमेव च ।
एतद्वेदितुमिच्छामि ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं च केशव ॥ १३-१॥
prakRtiM puruShan chaiva kShetran kShetrajnameva cha 
etadveditumichChAmi jnAnaM jneyan cha keshava  13.1

“O Keshava, I wish to understand what prakṛiti and puruṣha are, and the distinction between kṣhetra and kṣhetrajña. 
I also wish to know what true knowledge is, and the objective of such knowledge.

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
shrIbhagavAnuvAcha 

Lord Krishna then replied"

इदं शरीरं कौन्तेय क्षेत्रमित्यभिधीयते ।
एतद्यो वेत्ति तं प्राहुः क्षेत्रज्ञ इति तद्विदः ॥ १३-२॥
idaM sharIraM kaunteya kShetramityabhidhIyate 
etadyo vetti taM prAhuH kShetrajna iti tadvidaH  13.2

O Arjuna, this body is termed as kṣhetra, and the one who has the knowledge of residence in the body is called as kṣhetrajña, by the sages who discern the truth about both.

क्षेत्रज्ञं चापि मां विद्धि सर्वक्षेत्रेषु भारत ।
क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञयोर्ज्ञानं यत्तज्ज्ञानं मतं मम ॥ १३-३॥
kShetrajnan chApi mAM viddhi sarvakShetreShu bhArata 
kShetrakShetrajnayorjnAnaM yattajjnAnaM mataM mama  13.3

O descendant of Bharata, know that it is "I" who is the kshetrajna who resides in the kshetras of all beings.
The discernment between kshetra and kshetrajna is considered as true knowledge.

तत्क्षेत्रं यच्च यादृक्च यद्विकारि यतश्च यत् ।
स च यो यत्प्रभावश्च तत्समासेन मे श‍ृणु ॥ १३-४॥
tatkShetran yachcha yAdRkcha yadvikAri yatashcha yat 
sa cha yo yatprabhAvashcha tatsamAsena me shRNu  13.4

Listen now, as I give you the complete knowledge of  what that field is, what its nature is, how change takes place within it, and from what it is created.
I will also speak to you of who the knower of this field of activities is, and what the knower's purview is.

ऋषिभिर्बहुधा गीतं छन्दोभिर्विविधैः पृथक् ।
ब्रह्मसूत्रपदैश्चैव हेतुमद्भिर्विनिश्चितैः ॥ १३-५॥
RShibhirbahudhA gItan ChandobhirvividhaiH pRthak 
brahmasUtrapadaishchaiva hetumadbhirvinishchitaiH  13.5

This knowledge is what is spoken of by all the sages and documented in all of the Vedas, in various ways.This is the knowledge that the Brahma sutras talk about and the knowledge that the realized beings speak of.

महाभूतान्यहङ्कारो बुद्धिरव्यक्तमेव च ।
इन्द्रियाणि दशैकं च पञ्च चेन्द्रियगोचराः ॥ १३-६॥
mahAbhUtAnyahankAro buddhiravyaktameva cha 
indriyANi dashaikan cha pancha chendriyagocharAH  13.6

The five great elements, the ego, the intangible intellect, the eleven senses (five for impressions, five for expression, and the mind), and the five objects of these senses,

इच्छा द्वेषः सुखं दुःखं सङ्घातश्चेतना धृतिः ।
एतत्क्षेत्रं समासेन सविकारमुदाहृतम् ॥ १३-७॥
ichChA dveShaH sukhaM duHkhaM sanghAtashchetanA dhRtiH 
etatkShetran samAsena savikAramudAhRtam  13.7

Liking and aversion, happiness and misery, reverberance, the body, consciousness, and the will—all these comprise the kshetra and its modifications.

The next five shlokas describe various qualities that lord Krishna ascribes to true knowledge. He lists out:

अमानित्वमदम्भित्वमहिंसा क्षान्तिरार्जवम् ।
आचार्योपासनं शौचं स्थैर्यमात्मविनिग्रहः ॥ १३-८॥
amAnitvamadambhitvamahinsA kShAntirArjavam 
AchAryopAsanaM shauchaM sthairyamAtmavinigrahaH  13.8

Humbleness; freedom from hypocrisy; non-violence; forgiveness; simplicity; service of the Guru; cleanliness of body and mind; steadfastness; and self-control; 

इन्द्रियार्थेषु वैराग्यमनहङ्कार एव च ।
जन्ममृत्युजराव्याधिदुःखदोषानुदर्शनम् ॥ १३-९॥
indriyArtheShu vairAgyamanahankAra eva cha 
janmamRtyujarAvyAdhiduHkhadoShAnudarshanam  13.9

dispassion toward the objects of the senses; absence of egotism; keeping in mind the evils of birth, disease, old age, and death;

असक्तिरनभिष्वङ्गः पुत्रदारगृहादिषु ।
नित्यं च समचित्तत्वमिष्टानिष्टोपपत्तिषु ॥ १३-१०॥
asaktiranabhiShvangaH putradAragRhAdiShu 
nityan cha samachittatvamiShTAniShTopapattiShu  13.10

non-attachment; absence of clinging to spouse, children, home, and so on; even-mindedness amidst pleasant and unpleasant life situations; 

मयि चानन्ययोगेन भक्तिरव्यभिचारिणी ।
विविक्तदेशसेवित्वमरतिर्जनसंसदि ॥ १३-११॥
mayi chAnanyayogena bhaktiravyabhichAriNI 
viviktadeshasevitvamaratirjanasansadi  13.11

constant and exclusive devotion toward Me; an inclination for solitary places and an aversion for mundane society; 

अध्यात्मज्ञाननित्यत्वं तत्त्वज्ञानार्थदर्शनम् ।
एतज्ज्ञानमिति प्रोक्तमज्ञानं यदतोऽन्यथा ॥ १३-१२॥
adhyAtmajnAnanityatvan tattvajnAnArthadarshanam 
etajjnAnamiti proktamajnAnaM yadatoanyathA  13.12

Constant immersion in spiritual knowledge; and realisation of the eternal truth of unity—all these I declare to be knowledge, and what is contrary to it, I call ignorance.

ज्ञेयं यत्तत्प्रवक्ष्यामि यज्ज्ञात्वामृतमश्नुते ।
अनादिमत्परं ब्रह्म न सत्तन्नासदुच्यते ॥ १३-१३॥
jneyaM yattatpravakShyAmi yajjnAtvAmRtamashnute 
anAdimatparaM brahma na sattannAsaduchyate  13.13

I shall now reveal to you that which ought to be known, and by knowing which, one attains immortality. 
It is the beginningless Brahman, which lies beyond that which exists and that which doesn't.

सर्वतः पाणिपादं तत्सर्वतोऽक्षिशिरोमुखम् ।
सर्वतः श्रुतिमल्लोके सर्वमावृत्य तिष्ठति ॥ १३-१४॥
sarvataH pANipAdaM tatsarvatoakShishiromukham 
sarvataH shrutimalloke sarvamAvRtya tiShThati  13.14

It has its manifestations everywhere, with many many kinds of hands and feet, eyes, heads, and faces. 
Its ears are everywhere, and it pervades everything in the universe.

सर्वेन्द्रियगुणाभासं सर्वेन्द्रियविवर्जितम् ।
असक्तं सर्वभृच्चैव निर्गुणं गुणभोक्तृ च ॥ १३-१५॥
sarvendriyaguNAbhAsaM sarvendriyavivarjitam 
asaktan sarvabhRchchaiva nirguNaM guNabhoktR cha  13.15

Though the all pervading unity perceives all sense-objects, yet it is devoid of any sensory organs. 
It is not entangled with anything, and yet is the sustainer of all. 
Although the brahman is without attributes, it is the consumer of the three modes of material nature.

बहिरन्तश्च भूतानामचरं चरमेव च ।
सूक्ष्मत्वात्तदविज्ञेयं दूरस्थं चान्तिके च तत् ॥ १३-१६॥
bahirantashcha bhUtAnAmacharan charameva cha 
sUkShmatvAttadavijneyaM dUrasthan chAntike cha tat  13.16

It exists outside and inside all living beings, those that are moving and not moving. 
It is subtle, and hence, is incomprehensible at a physical level. 
It is very far with one kind of an approach, but is also very near with another.

अविभक्तं च भूतेषु विभक्तमिव च स्थितम् ।
भूतभर्तृ च तज्ज्ञेयं ग्रसिष्णु प्रभविष्णु च ॥ १३-१७॥
avibhaktan cha bhUteShu vibhaktamiva cha sthitam 
bhUtabhartR cha tajjneyaM grasiShNu prabhaviShNu cha  13.17

It is indivisible, yet appears to be distinct for each living being. 
Know the Supreme Entity to be the Sustainer, Annihilator, and Creator of all beings.

ज्योतिषामपि तज्ज्योतिस्तमसः परमुच्यते ।
ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं ज्ञानगम्यं हृदि सर्वस्य विष्ठितम् ॥ १३-१८॥
jyotiShAmapi tajjyotistamasaH paramuchyate 
jnAnaM jneyaM jnAnagamyan hRdi sarvasya viShThitam  13.18

It is the source of light in all luminaries, and is entirely beyond the darkness of ignorance. 
It is knowledge, the object of knowledge, and the goal of knowledge. 
It dwells within the hearts of all living beings.

इति क्षेत्रं तथा ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं चोक्तं समासतः ।
मद्भक्त एतद्विज्ञाय मद्भावायोपपद्यते ॥ १३-१९॥
iti kShetran tathA jnAnaM jneyan choktan samAsataH 
madbhakta etadvijnAya madbhAvAyopapadyate  13.19

I have thus revealed to you the nature of the body, the nature of true knowledge, and the objective of such knowledge. 
Only the devotees of Me can understand this in reality, and by doing so, they attain to My very own state of being.

प्रकृतिं पुरुषं चैव विद्ध्यनादी उभावपि ।
विकारांश्च गुणांश्चैव विद्धि प्रकृतिसम्भवान् ॥ १३-२०॥
prakRtiM puruShan chaiva vidhdhyanAdI ubhAvapi 
vikArAnshcha guNAnshchaiva viddhi prakRtisambhavAn  13.20

Know that prakṛiti,  the material energy of creation and puruṣha, the consciousness that drives it, are both without a beginning. 
Also know that all attributes and changes to those attributes are born out of prakRti.

कार्यकारणकर्तृत्वे हेतुः प्रकृतिरुच्यते ।
पुरुषः सुखदुःखानां भोक्तृत्वे हेतुरुच्यते ॥ १३-२१॥
kAryakAraNakartRtve hetuH prakRtiruchyate 
puruShaH sukhaduHkhAnAM bhoktRtve heturuchyate  13.21

In the matters of the need for activity, the driver of activity and the action itself, it is the material energy which is the cause; and in the matters of experience, like happiness and sorrow, it is the consciousness which is the cause.

पुरुषः प्रकृतिस्थो हि भुङ्क्ते प्रकृतिजान्गुणान् ।
कारणं गुणसङ्गोऽस्य सदसद्योनिजन्मसु ॥ १३-२२॥
puruShaH prakRtistho hi bhunhkte prakRtijAnguNAn 
kAraNaM guNasangoasya sadasadyonijanmasu  13.22

The consciousness, which is established within the material manifestation, consumes the effects and attributes born out of it.
Due to its entanglement with these attributes, it gets reborn in various kinds of wombs.

उपद्रष्टानुमन्ता च भर्ता भोक्ता महेश्वरः ।
परमात्मेति चाप्युक्तो देहेऽस्मिन्पुरुषः परः ॥ १३-२३॥
upadraShTAnumantA cha bhartA bhoktA maheshvaraH 
paramAtmeti chApyukto deheasminpuruShaH paraH  13.23

That which witnesses everything, allows these experiences to happen, sustains and consumes them is none other than the supreme divinity itself.
It resides within all material bodies, but is beyond the realm of the physical.

य एवं वेत्ति पुरुषं प्रकृतिं च गुणैः सह ।
सर्वथा वर्तमानोऽपि न स भूयोऽभिजायते ॥ १३-२४॥
ya evaM vetti puruShaM prakRtin cha guNaiH saha 
sarvathA vartamAnoapi na sa bhUyoabhijAyate  13.24

The one who knows this distinction between the consciousness, materialistic nature and its attributes, such a person who is fully present in each moment, doesn't get reborn again.

ध्यानेनात्मनि पश्यन्ति केचिदात्मानमात्मना ।
अन्ये साङ्ख्येन योगेन कर्मयोगेन चापरे ॥ १३-२५॥
dhyAnenAtmani pashyanti kechidAtmAnamAtmanA 
anye sAnkhyena yogena karmayogena chApare  13.25

Some try to perceive the true self within their own interiority through meditation, and others try to do so through the cultivation of knowledge and analyses, while yet others strive to attain that realization by the path of action.

अन्ये त्वेवमजानन्तः श्रुत्वान्येभ्य उपासते ।
तेऽपि चातितरन्त्येव मृत्युं श्रुतिपरायणाः ॥ १३-२६॥
anye tvevamajAnantaH shrutvAnyebhya upAsate 
teapi chAtitarantyeva mRtyun shrutiparAyaNAH  13.26

There are yet others who are unaware of this true nature of the self, but they hear from others and begin worshipping this Supreme unity. 
By such devotion towards what they've just head about, they too shall cross over the ocean of birth and death.

यावत्सञ्जायते किञ्चित्सत्त्वं स्थावरजङ्गमम् ।
क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञसंयोगात्तद्विद्धि भरतर्षभ ॥ १३-२७॥
yAvatsanjAyate kinchitsattvaM sthAvarajangamam 
kShetrakShetrajnasanyogAttadviddhi bharatarShabha  13.27

O best of the Bharatas, whatever is mobile or immobile in all of creation, know it to be a combination of kshetra, the materialistic manifestation and kshetrajna, the knower of it.

समं सर्वेषु भूतेषु तिष्ठन्तं परमेश्वरम् ।
विनश्यत्स्वविनश्यन्तं यः पश्यति स पश्यति ॥ १३-२८॥
samaM sarveShu bhUteShu tiShThantaM parameshvaram 
vinashyatsvavinashyantan yaH pashyati sa pashyati  13.28

The one who is equanimous and sees this unchanging Supreme divinity which is established in all of this ever changing creation, such a person is truly perceptive.

समं पश्यन्हि सर्वत्र समवस्थितमीश्वरम् ।
न हिनस्त्यात्मनात्मानं ततो याति परां गतिम् ॥ १३-२९॥
samaM pashyanhi sarvatra samavasthitamIshvaram 
na hinastyAtmanAtmAnaM tato yAti parAM gatim  13.29

A person who looks at everything with equanimity, identifies the supreme lord in all of creation and causes no harm to any being, such a person attains to the highest of abodes.

प्रकृत्यैव च कर्माणि क्रियमाणानि सर्वशः ।
यः पश्यति तथात्मानमकर्तारं स पश्यति ॥ १३-३०॥
prakRtyaiva cha karmANi kriyamANAni sarvashaH 
yaH pashyati tathAtmAnamakartAraM sa pashyati  13.30

The one who understands that all activity is born out of the materialistic nature, while the consciousness just stays a witness, untouched by this activity; such a person is truly perceptive.

यदा भूतपृथग्भावमेकस्थमनुपश्यति ।
तत एव च विस्तारं ब्रह्म सम्पद्यते तदा ॥ १३-३१॥
yadA bhUtapRthagbhAvamekasthamanupashyati 
tata eva cha vistAraM brahma sampadyate tadA  13.31

When a person perceives that the same single divinity is manifest as so many different aspects of creation, then one attains to the realisation of the true extent of the brahman.

अनादित्वान्निर्गुणत्वात्परमात्मायमव्ययः ।
शरीरस्थोऽपि कौन्तेय न करोति न लिप्यते ॥ १३-३२॥
anAditvAnnirguNatvAtparamAtmAyamavyayaH 
sharIrasthoapi kaunteya na karoti na lipyate  13.32

The true self is imperishable, without beginning, and devoid of any material qualities, O son of Kunti. Although situated within the body, it neither acts, nor is it tainted by materialistic nature.

यथा सर्वगतं सौक्ष्म्यादाकाशं नोपलिप्यते ।
सर्वत्रावस्थितो देहे तथात्मा नोपलिप्यते ॥ १३-३३॥
yathA sarvagataM saukShmyAdAkAshaM nopalipyate 
sarvatrAvasthito dehe tathAtmA nopalipyate  13.33

Just as the space that holds even the subtlest of elements is not smeared by it, similarly even by pervading the entire existence, the true self is not smeared by it.

यथा प्रकाशयत्येकः कृत्स्नं लोकमिमं रविः ।
क्षेत्रं क्षेत्री तथा कृत्स्नं प्रकाशयति भारत ॥ १३-३४॥
yathA prakAshayatyekaH kRtsnan lokamimaM raviH 
kShetran kShetrI tathA kRtsnaM prakAshayati bhArata  13.34

Just as one sun illuminates the entire world, so does the consciousness illuminate the entire body O Arjuna.

क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञयोरेवमन्तरं ज्ञानचक्षुषा ।
भूतप्रकृतिमोक्षं च ये विदुर्यान्ति ते परम् ॥ १३-३५॥
kShetrakShetrajnayorevamantaraM jnAnachakShuShA 
bhUtaprakRtimokShan cha ye viduryAnti te param  13.35

Those who perceive with the eyes of knowledge the difference between the kShetra and the kShetrajna, and the process to get liberated from the cycles of materialistic nature, they attain the supreme destination.

ॐ तत्सदिति श्रीमद्भगवद्गीतासूपनिषत्सु
ब्रह्मविद्यायां योगशास्त्रे श्रीकृष्णार्जुनसंवादे
क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञविभागयोगो नाम त्रयोदशोऽध्यायः ॥ १३॥
AUM tatsaditi shrImadbhagavadgItAsUpaniShatsu
brahmavidyAyAn yogashAstre shrIkRShNArjunasanvAde
kShetrakShetrajnavibhAgayogo nAma trayodashoadhyAyaH  13

And thus ends the thirteenth chapter, by the name of kShetrakShetrajnavibhAga yogaH.
This is taken from Shrimad Bhagavad Gita, a conversation between Lord Krishna and Arjuna on the science of yoga, which is the knowledge of ultimate reality, called as brahma vidya.
